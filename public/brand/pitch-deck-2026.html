The Problem
Most software was built
for the 20% who work in tech.

Project management software is designed for engineering teams — sprint boards, story points, Gantt charts, integrations with GitHub. The people actually running most of the world's important work — venue coordinators, wedding planners, tradespeople, freelancers, small-business owners — are handed the same tools and told to figure it out.

The result is coordination by WhatsApp thread, Excel sheet, and repeated email. Work gets lost. People ask the same question twelve times. The person responsible for the outcome is never sure what state anything is in.

80% of the working world is not in tech. The tools they've been given were not designed for them — they were designed for someone else, then left on the shelf for everyone to use.

The Solution
One suite. Four layers.
Everything important. Nothing distracting.

Notes is where it starts. Tasks is where it gets done. Roadmap is what everyone else sees. Analytics tells you where to look.

Signal Notes
where work begins
Private context capture. Nothing leaves until you send it.
A place to write what you know before you know what to do with it. One tap moves a note into a task. Notes never auto-route, auto-detect, or auto-share.
Signal Tasks
where work happens
The operational engine. Plain language, no jargon.
Multi-view task workspace — list, board, calendar. No "story points." No "sprints." Works for a couple planning a wedding just as well as a contractor managing a build.
Signal Roadmap
what people see
The shareable public artifact. No account required.
A plain-English project page anyone can open. The couple's suppliers see it. The venue's coordinator sees it. The person outside the workspace who needs to know what's happening — they get a link, not a login.
Signal Analytics
what needs attention
A morning briefing, not a dashboard. Three things, in plain English.
Delivered once a day. No charts to interpret, no filters to set. Tells you what needs attention right now. Silence is the signal — no briefing means nothing is urgent.

Go-to-Market · Commercial Wedge
One venue signature.
A cohort of activated users.

A premium wedding or events venue pays once a year — €1,500 to €4,000, prepaid. For that, every couple they book gets a Signal Studio workspace with the venue's name as a quiet eyebrow. The couple plans their event inside it.

The venue's team does not operate the software. Their only verbs are: settle the invoice, hand out access codes, do one short annual retro.

One commercial decision at the venue unlocks a cohort of real end-users — each couple becomes a workspace. Every couple who has a calm planning experience reflects back on the venue's brand.

"Fewer confused couples. Fewer repetitive emails. A better planning experience under your venue's name."
