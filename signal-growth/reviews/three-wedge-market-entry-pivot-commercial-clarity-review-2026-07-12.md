# Three-Wedge Market Entry Pivot Commercial Clarity Review

## Findings

1. The old 18-venue Year 1 target was an underwriting case presented as a commercial ceiling. It hid a five-market opportunity of 278,956 latest final marriages and more than 10,000 official-source venue records and account proxies.
2. A 100-venue target is ambitious but legible: 3,000 contacted accounts, 600 qualified, 400 demos, 250 proposals or structured pilots, and 100 wins. It requires roughly two to three wins per week across the active selling year.
3. Student and teacher upside is real only when the metrics stay separate. The plan should report paid students, paid schools, activated teachers, retention, and billings rather than combining them as generic licences.

## Decisions Recommended

1. Ratify the Year 1 operating target at 100 paid venues, 7,500 paid students, and 30 paid schools, equal to €269,925 in gross contract value and billings before VAT, fees, refunds, and revenue-recognition timing.
2. Keep 18 paid venues and €27,000 as the lender underwriting floor and Q1 proof gate. Release revenue-funded commercial capacity only after this gate, then treat 150 venues, 20,000 students, and 75 schools as the capacity-backed stretch case.

## Risks

1. A founder-only operating model will miss the target through capacity, not market size. Lead qualification, demos, onboarding, and follow-up need a documented system and added commercial support after Q1 proof.
2. School Edition must remain teacher-only in Year 1 with zero pupil accounts and zero pupil personal data. Currency, VAT, data-processing, procurement, and safeguarding terms need explicit review before school contracts are sold.

## Tasks

1. Build the 3,000-account venue CRM allocation: England 1,650; Ireland 500; Scotland 450; Wales 250; Northern Ireland 150.
2. Instrument separate paid acquisition and retention ledgers for 7,500 students and the 30-school teacher-only offer.
3. Complete School Edition commercial terms, privacy boundaries, procurement pack, and the revenue-funded commercial hiring gate.

## Keep / Change / Stop

Keep:

- Limerick as the proof beachhead, the fixed €1,500 Venue Edition price, prepaid contracts, and an 18-venue downside cash model.

Change:

- Treat Ireland and the UK's four nations as the Year 1 market, make the three wedges explicit, and show operating target, underwriting floor, and stretch case separately.

Stop:

- Presenting a proof cohort, a local CRM count, or an underwriting floor as the company's maximum commercial ambition.
