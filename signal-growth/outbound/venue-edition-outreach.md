## The reconciliation you need to read before sending

The prospect list says "sequence group commercial leads before property cold-email." The ratified Venue Edition strategy says the pilot proof-gate is **an owner-operator who can sign alone** — a venue tied to a hotel group's procurement chain is an explicit disqualifier for the pilot stage (`VENUE_EDITION_STRATEGY.md` qualification + spine §7 risk 2).

These are not in conflict once you read the group-play stance for what it was: an **efficiency** rule (don't cold-email three Doyle Collection properties separately — talk to Doyle once), not a priority rule. So the set splits into two tracks:

- **Track A — Independent, owner-operable wedding hotels. This is the pilot.** They can sign alone. Metric 3 (one paid pilot in 60 days) closes here or it does not close. These get the founder email first, and the follow-ups.
- **Track B — Group commercial leads. One email per group, not per property. Efficiency track, explicitly NOT counted toward the 60-day kill trigger.** A group conversation can unlock several properties later. It will not close in the pilot window, and treating a group stall as a thesis failure would misread the market.

**Dissent, named:** if you send Track B first because there are more properties behind it, you will spend the 60-day window on conversations that structurally cannot close, and the kill trigger will fire on the wrong evidence. Track A is the priority. Track B runs in parallel, slower, and is read separately.

## Send discipline (from the spine, §5)

Founder-signed. One at a time. No automation, no drip tool. The two follow-ups below are **manual** — a person deciding to write again, not a sequence firing. That is the whole point of the register; an automated cadence would contradict the product.

- Send from `hello@signalstudio.ie`. Test-send to yourself first (DKIM still pending per `project_email.md` — check it does not land in spam).
- One personalised sentence per send. Load-bearing. If the sentence could go to a different venue unedited, it is the wrong sentence.
- Log every send / reply / call in the `/hq` Outbound CRM against the prospect row.
- No social proof. No venue has signed. Claiming or implying otherwise breaks the one thing the brand is built on.
- No urgency theatre. "Founding fifteen" is a real constraint stated plainly, never "limited spots, act now."

## Voice guard (run before every send)

- No exclamation marks, anywhere.
- No "AI", "intelligent", "automatically", "seamless", "leverage", "robust", "stakeholder", "sprint", "MVP".
- No three-adjective trios.
- Wordmark `Signal Studio` in body, never bare "Signal".
- Reads like a person wrote it to one named owner. If it reads like a SaaS sequence, it is wrong.

## What this set deliberately does not do

- No social proof — no venue has signed.
- No urgency framing — "founding fifteen" is a fact, not a scarcity trick.
- No automated sequence — the follow-ups are a person choosing to write again.
- No pricing softening — €1,500 founding is the offer, stated once, not negotiated down in the cold email.
- No Track B follow-up cadence — one founder note to a group, then wait. Chasing a procurement chain by email is the failure mode the pilot is designed to avoid.
