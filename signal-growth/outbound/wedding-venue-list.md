# Wedding Venue List · 2026-05-15 input gate

**Owner**: Ethan · **Deadline**: 2026-05-15 (Friday) · **Status**: assembling

Ten Irish wedding venues. Each entry must have a coordinator name, an email address, and one specific reason-this-venue. If a reason can't be written, the venue doesn't go on the list — generic "wedding venue in Cork" entries dilute the personalisation that earns replies (see `wedding-venue-outreach-kit.md` → Personalisation rule).

**Reason-this-venue test**: could this sentence be sent to any other venue without editing? If yes, it's not specific enough.

If 10 named entries with passing reason-sentences can't be assembled by 2026-05-15, polish work stops and the next cycle becomes "why can't we name 10 venues" — that's the venue-pilot-forcing-function HQ decision in action.

## Sourcing notes (where to look)

- **Instagram hashtags**: `#irishwedding`, `#weddingvenueireland`, `#wedding[county]`. Look at the venue's own posts — coordinator name often credited.
- **Wedding magazines**: *Confetti*, *One Fab Day*, *Wedding Journal Ireland*. Real recent features beat generic "top 10 venues" listicles.
- **Hashtag-real-people**: real couples' wedding hashtags reveal venues that get repeat bookings. A venue that runs 25 weddings/year has 25 hashtags' worth of evidence.
- **Personal recommendations**: friends in the wedding industry. Highest reply-rate source by far. Worth two days of texting friends before assembling the list from cold sources.

---

## Geographic spread (sanity check)

Don't ship 10 Dublin venues. Aim roughly:
- 3 Dublin / Wicklow / Kildare
- 3 Cork / Kerry / Waterford
- 2 Galway / Clare / Mayo
- 2 anywhere else (Donegal, Tipperary, Wexford, Kilkenny, etc.)

If reply rates concentrate in one region, the campaign learns something useful about regional product-market fit. If the list is monoculture, it doesn't.

---

## Capacity spread (sanity check)

Outreach kit targets 60–200 capacity venues. Within that:
- 4 in the 60–100 range (boutique, country house, restored barn)
- 4 in the 100–150 range (mid-size country house, hotel ballroom)
- 2 in the 150–200 range (large hotel, country estate)

Different size venues run different coordination patterns. The pilot wants to learn across the size axis.
