# Weekly Operating Model

The marketing engine is founder-led, AI-assisted, and manual at the point of contact. No paid ads. No mass sequence. No daily content treadmill.

## Monday

- Build or clean the next 15-20 venue prospects.
- Clear the approval queue: outreach, SEO briefs, video scripts, and Dispatch candidates.
- Pick the one asset that ships this week.
- Log the one-line KPI state: sent, replies, calls, paid conversations, redemptions.

## Tuesday

- Send 8-10 founder-reviewed venue emails.
- Personalise one sentence per venue.
- Log every send with the standard tracking shape:
  `source`, `campaign`, `audience`, `artifact`, `touch`, `venue`.

## Wednesday

- Send the second 8-10 founder-reviewed venue emails.
- Record or edit one demo clip.
- Keep the clip tied to real product screens, not abstract motion.

## Thursday

- Reply to prospects.
- Send manual follow-ups only where a person would reasonably write again.
- Speak to users, venues, planners, or operators.

## Friday

- Review metrics and objections.
- Tighten one landing page, one email paragraph, or one demo beat based on what came back.
- Add one Dispatch candidate only if something shipped that a venue operator could understand.

## Weekend

- Improve one compounding surface: `/venues`, `/weddings`, one comparison page, one template page, or one demo route.

## Monthly Rhythm

- Two strong videos: one product proof, one founder/refusal/brand piece.
- Two comparison or intent pages.
- One case-study or proof-scene improvement.
- One asset-library refresh.

## Kill Or Iterate

- Fewer than 5 replies from 50 good venue emails: targeting or first paragraph is wrong.
- Calls but no paid movement: the one-pager or price story is not clear enough.
- Paid venue but low couple activation: handoff artifact or redemption flow is wrong.
- Any asset that needs hype, fake urgency, or AI-marketing language to work is killed.
