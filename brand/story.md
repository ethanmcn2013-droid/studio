# The last tool you'll have to learn

Most software wants more of you. More setup. More fields. More words to learn
before you can do the thing you came to do. Signal Studio wants less.

It started with a person, not a market. A wedding planner with a date to hold. A
builder with three sites and a phone full of half-typed notes. A student carrying
a whole term in her head. A freelancer chasing five clients through one inbox.
None of them asked for a workflow. They have work. They just want to see it.

So we built four small tools that read as one system. Notes catches the thought
before it fades. Tasks runs the work without the project-manager voice. Timeline
is the one page everyone else can read, no login, no app. Signal reads it all
overnight and hands you a paragraph in the morning. When nothing moved, it says
nothing. Silence is the signal.

There are no sprints here. No stand-ups. No one asking for your velocity. No
certificate to earn before you are allowed to be organised. If it needs a
tutorial, we failed. Open the page and it already makes sense.

The hard part was never what we added. It was what we left out. Every feature we
refused is a feature you never have to understand. The moat is what we won't
build.

Made by one person, in Limerick, on purpose. It ships slowly because it ships for
years. Less, but better. Built for the 80% who run real work and never wanted to
run software.

Built for everyone else.
