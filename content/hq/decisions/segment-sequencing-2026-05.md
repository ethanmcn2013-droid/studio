## Decision

A 6-lens agent panel (market-math, day-to-day utility, wedge & moat, brand fit, adoption reality, cost to serve) scored five segments against the venue benchmark. **The founder accepted the consensus in full on 2026-05-16.** It is now a standing directional constraint to prevent drift.

**Binding sequencing:**
1. **Venues only — now → +30 days.** No new segment work. The §8 venue kill/pivot gate is not pre-empted. This decision changes nothing about current focus.
2. **Contractors / trades = the designated second wedge**, activated only if venues prove (≥1 paid pilot + couples activate). It is the only non-wedding segment that replicates both wedge halves: a buyer who activates a cohort (a general contractor signing for subs) and a forwardable client-facing artifact (a build-progress Timeline to the homeowner). Test the GC-activates-subs buyer early — that is its most likely failure point.
3. **Small-business = opportunistic inbound, never a planned paid wedge.** Highest raw revenue ceiling (~€3.6–7.2M) and best full-suite daily fit, but no cohort buyer → linear funnel growth that strains the brand. Revisit only if a cohort buyer surfaces (hospitality group, clinic network).
4. **Teachers & students = audience truth in BRAND.md §2.1 only.** No outbound, no demos, no pilots, no dedicated paid push, no segment landing page built on population grounds. Split the student archetype: postgrad/professional is the real fit; general undergraduate loses to free Notion before the voice speaks — do not build for it.
5. **Compliance fence (decide before any "school"/"classroom" surface ships):** age-fence the entitlement source to adults, zero pupil PII. Positioning decision, not a code change.

## Reason

The founder's prior instinct was "more teachers than hotels → teachers scale better." The panel showed that population is the wrong axis: the number that scales revenue is *payable × adoptable × reachable-by-a-solo-founder × has-a-buyer-who-can-sign-alone*. On that number venues beat teachers by an order of magnitude. Teachers fail four of six lenses — free incumbents (Notion is €0 for teachers vs our €144/yr), no buyer who can sign alone (schools are 18–24-month institutional procurement), institutional tool mandates that confine us to the job's overflow scraps, and an under-18 data-compliance regime (FERPA + GDPR Art. 8) a solo founder must not carry. The decisive argument: chasing a low-money/no-buyer/free-incumbent segment forces volume conversion → a self-serve marketing funnel → the exact machinery BRAND.md §2.2–§2.3 defines the company against. The venue wedge fails cheaply and reversibly; the teacher motion fails expensively and identity-destroyingly. The population argument inverts the moat.

## Risks

The standing risk this decision exists to prevent is **drift**: a future cycle re-encountering the large teacher/student TAM and re-litigating it on headcount. Mitigation: this file + the RATIFIED banner on SEGMENT_EXPANSION_RESEARCH_2026_05.md + the memory tripwire are the canonical references — any segment-expansion proposal must cite and clear them first. Secondary risk: the contractor second-wedge buyer (GC-activates-subs) is less proven than the venue buyer was; do not assume trades is cleaner than weddings — test the buyer relationship explicitly before committing outreach.
