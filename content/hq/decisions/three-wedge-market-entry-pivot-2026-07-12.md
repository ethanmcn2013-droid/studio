## Decision

Signal Studio moves from a reserved Ireland-only venue plan to an ambitious, evidence-gated Year 1 market entry across wedding venues, higher-education students, and secondary-school teachers. Commercial Year 1 runs from 1 September 2026 to 31 August 2027.

The operating target is:

| Wedge | Year 1 target | Price | Gross contract value or billings |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Venue Edition | 100 paid venues | €1,500 per venue per year, prepaid | €150,000 |
| Student Edition | 7,500 paid students | €9.99 per student per year | €74,925 |
| School Edition | 30 paid schools | €1,500 per school per year, prepaid | €45,000 |
| Total | 100 venues, 7,500 students, 30 schools |  | €269,925 |

These are gross commercial values before VAT, currency conversion, processor fees, refunds, and revenue-recognition timing. The lender cash model remains separate, runs from 1 July 2026 to 30 June 2027, and assumes only 18 paid venues and €27,000. The management plan separately requires 18 cumulative paid venues by 30 November 2026. Eighteen is a proof gate and underwriting floor, not the Year 1 ceiling.

The capacity-backed stretch case is 150 venues, 20,000 students, and 75 schools, equal to €537,300. It is conditional on productized onboarding and added commercial or partnerships capacity after the 18-venue proof gate. It is not the lender forecast.

## Market evidence

- Latest final marriages across the five markets total 278,956: England and Wales use 2023 ONS data; Ireland, Scotland, and Northern Ireland use 2024 CSO, NRS, and NISRA data.
- More than 10,000 official-source venue records and account proxies are available for qualification. They are a discovery universe, not a count of confirmed active wedding venues.
- The initial UK and Ireland undergraduate serviceable market is 2,278,975 enrolments from HESA and HEA 2024/25. The 7,500 paid target is 0.33%.
- The core secondary and post-primary account universe is 4,903 schools. The 30-school target is 0.61%. The approximately 298,000 teacher opportunity combines different official measures and is directional, not harmonized.

## Commercial system

The venue funnel plans 3,000 contacts, 600 qualified opportunities, 400 demos, 250 proposals or structured pilots, and 100 paid venues. The planned contact allocation is England 1,650; Ireland 500; Scotland 450; Wales 250; Northern Ireland 150. More than 500 Irish venues are mapped in the current founder CRM. The remaining contacts are a Year 1 expansion plan, not current pipeline.

The first fifteen paid Limerick venues form the founding feedback cohort and keep the fixed €1,500 price for life. That cohort is an evidence layer, not the commercial ceiling; the Year 1 venue target remains 100.

Student acquisition targets 4,500 paid users through 30 campus cells, 2,000 through 100,000 qualified visits at 2% checkout conversion, and 1,000 through ten partner cohorts. The sum represents 7,500 deduplicated paid accounts.

The direct school funnel is 250 qualified schools, 125 demos, and 30 paid schools. School Edition is a planned teacher-only workspace for up to 60 verified staff. It must have zero pupil accounts and zero pupil personal data in Year 1.

## Guardrails

1. Limerick remains the proof beachhead, not the market ceiling.
2. Venue Edition remains a hard €1,500 yearly prepaid price with no size band.
3. Paid venues, paid students, paid schools, and activated teachers stay separate in reporting.
4. No pupil data or pupil accounts enter School Edition before explicit product, legal, privacy, safeguarding, and procurement approval.
5. No deck may present addressable market records as qualified pipeline.
6. The 150-venue, 20,000-student, and 75-school case requires added capacity and remains conditional.
