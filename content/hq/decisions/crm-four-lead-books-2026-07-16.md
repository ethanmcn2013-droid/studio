## Decision

The CRM is four lead books, one per outbound motion: venues, students, schools, small business. A lead belongs to exactly one book. Each book carries its own offer, buyer definition, playbook line, and stage vocabulary. The proof gate reads the venue book only.

## Reason

The market-entry deck targets each audience with a different offer at a different price through a different channel: Venue Edition (€1,500/yr, phone-first founder outreach), Student Edition (€9.99/yr, campus cells at UL, TUS, MIC), School Edition (€1,500/school/yr, teacher discovery kit, zero pupil data), and the conditional small-business inbound wedge. One flat list forces one message onto four buyers and lets non-venue sends pollute the paid-venue proof gate.

## Alternatives considered

Keep one list with a filter. Rejected: a filter is an opt-in view; the books make the separation structural — different stage labels, different compose subjects, different empty states, separate lock-down scoreboards.

## Risks

Book discipline decays if new leads land with the wrong segment key. The seed normaliser maps legacy strings to the venue book; new records must use the four keys. The smb book stays empty by design until the Phase 5 wedge decision (Jan–Feb 2027) — filling it early is a red flag, not progress.
