# Founder Letter — Mam (Founding Member)
## Phase 1 · Two Drafts + Recommendation
*Version 1.0 — 2026-05-30*

---

## Notes on this letter

Mam's letter is different in kind, not just in degree.

The Founding Member designation is hers alone. That needs to be present in the letter — not announced, but felt. The certificate says it formally. The letter should say it personally.

The tone here is slower. More honest. The specific things she gave — time, patience, trust without evidence, presence while building — these are named directly. One sentence in this letter should be impossible to address to anyone else.

What to avoid: performing gratitude. "I cannot thank you enough" — no. "You have always believed in me" — too abstract, too generic. Name the actual thing. The actual thing is: she made space for work that had no guarantee. She did this not because she understood the business, but because she trusted the person building it. That is rare. That is what gets named.

---

## DRAFT A — Direct / Quietly personal

*Register: the quietest of all the letters. Personal without sentimentality. Direct about what was given without cataloguing it. Ends simply.*

---

September 1

Mam,

Signal Studio launched today.

You have known I was building something for a long time. You did not always know what it was — I did not always explain it well, and you did not always ask. You made space for it anyway, which is a different kind of support from the kind that asks questions.

This package contains a share certificate. Founding Member — the only one. Ten percent of Signal Studio, Class B: you participate in dividends, in the value of this business, in any outcome if it is ever sold or acquired. This is real, and it is permanent. The handbook explains what it means.

I did not include you because you are my mother. I included you because of how specifically and quietly you have allowed me to work on things that had no obvious return and no fixed timeline. That is harder to give than money or advice, and I have not always said so directly.

The decisions in this business remain mine. But whatever Signal Studio becomes, you share in it.

Ethan

## DRAFT B — Slightly more personal / names the specific gift more fully

*Register: the same restraint, but opens one degree further. Names the particular kind of trust she gave — not belief in the business, but in the person. That distinction is the emotional centre.*

---

September 1

Mam,

Signal Studio launched today.

You have known I was building something. Not always what it was — I did not always explain it clearly, and I think you sensed that pushing would not help. So you did not push. You trusted it, or more accurately, you trusted me, which is not quite the same thing, and means more.

This package contains a share certificate. Founding Member: the only designation of this kind. Ten percent of Signal Studio, Class B — dividend participation, a share of the value of this business, a share of any acquisition outcome. This is a real legal position, not a gesture. The handbook in this package explains it plainly.

I chose you for this before I chose anyone else. Not because you are my mother, though you are. Because over the time I have been building this, you have given me the rarer thing: the space to fail quietly if it came to that, without it costing either of us the relationship. That is not nothing. That is, in fact, the thing.

Signal Studio will be founder-led. The decisions remain mine. But what it becomes, you are part of.

Ethan

---

## Recommendation

**Draft B.** It earns more. The line "you trusted it, or more accurately, you trusted me, which is not quite the same thing, and means more" does what no other line in the letter system does — it distinguishes between two types of trust and names the one that matters. That sentence could only be written to a parent.

"Because over the time I have been building this, you have given me the rarer thing: the space to fail quietly if it came to that, without it costing either of us the relationship. That is not nothing. That is, in fact, the thing." — this paragraph names the specific gift without romanticising it. It is honest about the risk (failure) without dwelling on it. It is the emotional centre and it earns its place.

Draft A is strong but safer. If Draft B feels like too much when Ethan reads it, Draft A is the fallback. The instruction: read both aloud before deciding.
