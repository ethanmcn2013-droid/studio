# Dividend Philosophy
## Signal Studio Founder Circle
*Version 1.0 — 2026-05-30*

---

## Governing Principles

**1. Distributions are earned, not scheduled.**

There is no fixed payment date and no guaranteed minimum. Distributions happen when Signal Studio has genuine profit to distribute and the business is in a position to do so safely. A month with strong revenue does not automatically produce a distribution — profit is what remains after the business has taken care of itself.

**2. The operating reserve protects everyone.**

Before any distribution is considered, Signal Studio maintains a minimum operating reserve equivalent to three months of operating expenses. This reserve is not Ethan's to draw from. It is the baseline that protects the business — and therefore the value of everyone's shares — against a bad quarter, an unexpected cost, or a slow month.

Once the reserve is funded, additions to it drop to zero and distributable profit increases accordingly.

**3. Growth investment is founder-determined.**

After tax and after the reserve is healthy, Ethan determines what percentage of remaining profit is reinvested in growth — product development, infrastructure, marketing, or other investment. This is not arbitrary. The percentage will be higher in the early years of the business, when every reinvested euro compounds, and lower in mature years, when the infrastructure is built and revenue is stable.

Expected range: 15–30% of post-tax profit in years 1–4. Expected to fall toward 10–15% from year 5 onward as the business matures.

This reinvestment is what builds enterprise value — the value of everyone's shares in a sale or acquisition scenario. A business that distributes everything before it is ready to is worth less, not more.

**4. Transparency over performance management.**

The quarterly report is not written to project confidence or manage expectations. It reports what happened, including the quarters where no distribution is made and the honest reason why. Recipients deserve a real account.

**5. Distributions are paid promptly.**

When a distribution decision is made, payment is made to all Founder Circle members within 14 working days via bank transfer to their nominated account. There is no delay and no complication.

**6. No distribution in deficit.**

If Signal Studio is operating at a loss in a given quarter, no distribution is made. Full stop. Operating reserves exist for business stability, not for smoothing distributions.

**7. The arc is long.**

In the first two to three years of building a business without outside investment, distributions may be zero or minimal. This is expected and is not a sign that the business is failing. The value being created during this period is in the enterprise value of the shares — what they would be worth if the business were sold. That value is being built whether or not it is being distributed.

Recipients should expect meaningful regular distributions to begin when Signal Studio reaches sustained profitability — likely in year 2 or year 3. Smaller or zero distributions before that are the normal state of a growing self-funded business.

---

## The Relationship Between Growth and Distribution

These are not competing interests — they are sequenced interests.

In the early years, growth investment comes first. Every euro reinvested in a product that gets better compounds into higher revenue and higher enterprise value. That compounding is what makes the shares worth holding.

As the business matures and the growth investment required decreases (because the infrastructure is built and the revenue base is established), the distributable pool grows. The transition is gradual, not a cliff.

The founder will communicate transparently about where the business sits in this arc in each quarterly report.

---

## What "Distributable Profit" Means

Distributable profit is what remains after the business has fully funded itself for the next period. It is not a number that can be inflated by deferring costs or deflated by routing expenses through the business. The calculation is transparent and consistent quarter over quarter.

It is the honest answer to the question: "What did Signal Studio actually earn this quarter that it does not need in order to keep running well?"

---

## Long-Term Distribution Target (Illustrative, Not Binding)

Once Signal Studio reaches a sustainable revenue base, the target distribution percentage is:

| Year        | Growth Investment | Distributable Pool |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Year 1      | 30–40%            | 0–20% (reserve building) |
| Year 2      | 25–30%            | 20–35%             |
| Year 3      | 20–25%            | 35–50%             |
| Year 4+     | 10–20%            | 50–70%             |

These are indicative ranges, not commitments. The actual percentage in any given quarter depends on the business's real position.
