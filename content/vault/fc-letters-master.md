# Founder Letter — Master Template
## Phase 1 · Two Drafts + Recommendation
*Version 1.0 — 2026-05-30*

---

## DRAFT A — Restrained / Declarative

*Register: quiet, direct, no sentiment performed. Every statement is a fact. The emotion comes from accumulation, not from any single emotional word.*

---

September 1

[Name],

Signal Studio launched today.

I have been building it for the better part of a year, quietly. You knew some version of this — enough to know it was real, or to treat it as though it might be. Most people did not.

This package contains a share certificate. Founder Circle membership. Class B: you participate in dividends, in the value of this business, in any outcome if Signal Studio is ever acquired. Two and a half percent. The handbook in this package explains what that means in plain terms.

[Personalized paragraph — specific to this person. See FRIENDS.md for variants.]

I will run Signal Studio as a founder. The decisions are mine. But whatever it becomes, you are part of it.

A quarterly note will come — a real account of how things are going, not a performance. Once a year, something more considered.

Ethan

## DRAFT B — Slightly warmer / more personal

*Register: same restraint, but the voice opens slightly. Acknowledges the human relationship more directly. More suitable for close friends than formal ones.*

---

September 1

[Name],

Signal Studio launched today.

I have been building it quietly, for longer than most people knew. You knew some of this — not all of it. That was fine. The work needed to happen before there was anything to announce.

This package contains a share certificate. Class B Founder Circle membership: dividend participation, a share of the value of this business, a share of any outcome if it is ever sold or acquired. Two and a half percent. The handbook explains what that means.

[Personalized paragraph — specific to this person. See FRIENDS.md for variants.]

I will send you a brief note every quarter — not investor relations, just a real account of how things are going. Once a year, something more substantial.

Signal Studio is founder-led. The decisions remain mine. But the upside — whatever it becomes — you share in.

Ethan

---

## Recommendation

**Draft A for friends who are more reserved, formal, or where the relationship has a professional element.**
**Draft B for close friends where warmth is natural and won't read as performed.**

The two drafts are close. The real difference is in the third line of the final forward-looking paragraph. Draft A: "But whatever it becomes, you are part of it." Draft B: "But the upside — whatever it becomes — you share in."

Draft A is slightly more inclusive ("part of it" = belonging); Draft B is slightly more specific ("share in" = financial precision). Lean Draft A for emotional warmth, Draft B where precision is valued.

Mam's letter is a separate document — she gets neither of these templates.
