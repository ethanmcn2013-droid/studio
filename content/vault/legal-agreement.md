## Background

A. The Founder is building Signal Studio: a small suite of software products made for the people who run real work without a technical team — event planners, venue coordinators, tradespeople, small-business operators, and the many others the rest of the software industry overlooked. The Company's mission and vision are set out in **Schedule 3** and form part of the spirit of this Agreement.

B. Over the time the Founder has spent building Signal Studio, the Founding Member gave something that cannot be invoiced: trust given before there was any evidence to justify it, and the space to build something with no guaranteed outcome. This Agreement exists to make the Founding Member a real owner of what that trust helped create.

C. The Founder wishes to **gift** to the Founding Member a ten percent (10%) economic interest in Signal Studio, in the form of Class B Founder Circle Shares, on the terms set out below. The grant is made for natural love and affection and for no monetary consideration.

D. The Parties intend that Signal Studio remains **founder-led**: the Founding Member shares fully in the financial upside of the business while the Founder retains control of how it is run.

## 2. The Grant

2.1 The Founder agrees, on and from the Agreement Date, to grant and transfer to the Founding Member, as an absolute gift, **100,000 Class B Founder Circle Shares** in the Company. The grant takes effect and completes on **Completion** — when the Company is incorporated and the Shares are issued and registered in her name. 2.2 On Completion the issued share capital of the Company is **1,000,000 shares**. The Shares therefore represent **ten percent (10.00%)** of the issued share capital. The fixed number — 100,000 — is the operative figure; the percentage is stated for understanding and is protected by clause 8.

2.3 The grant is made for **no consideration**, for natural love and affection. The Founding Member is not required to pay for the Shares, now or ever.

2.4 The Founding Member's certificate number is **SS-FC-M**, and her designation is **Founding Member** — the only holder of that designation.

> **In plain terms.** You are being given 100,000 shares, which is 10% of Signal Studio. You pay nothing. They become yours on Completion — the moment the company is incorporated (expected July 2026) and the shares are registered in your name.

## 3. What your shares carry

3.1 The Class B Shares carry the following **economic rights**, in proportion to the 10% they represent:

(a) **Dividend participation** — the right to receive 10% of any Distributable Profit the Company distributes (clause 5);

(b) **Capital / value participation** — the right to receive 10% of the Net Proceeds in an Acquisition (clause 15), and to be told the Company's value each quarter (clause 7);

(c) **Information** — the quarterly report and annual communication described in clause 6;

(d) **Anti-dilution protection** — the maintenance of the 10% interest described in clause 8;

(e) **Right of first offer on a sale and tag-along** — the protections in clauses 12 and 14.

3.2 The Class B Shares **rank equally with all other shares** for dividends and for proceeds on an Acquisition, in proportion to their percentage. They are not deferred or subordinated.

## 4. What your shares do not carry

4.1 The Class B Shares are **non-voting**. They do not carry:

(a) the right to vote at general meetings (except where Irish company law gives a non-voting share a mandatory vote — for example, on a variation of the rights attaching to the Class B Shares themselves);

(b) any right to manage, direct, or participate in the day-to-day operation of the Company;

(c) any right to appoint a director or to board representation;

(d) any veto over, or power to block, any decision of the Company or the Founder.

4.2 Signal Studio is founder-led by design. The Founder makes the strategic and operational decisions. This is acknowledged and accepted as the basis of the grant.

> **In plain terms.** You share fully in the money. You do not run the company and you cannot be outvoted into responsibility for it. That is deliberate, and it protects you as much as it protects the business.

## 5. Dividends and distributions

5.1 **No guarantee.** Dividends depend on profit, and profit is not guaranteed — least of all in the early years of a business built without outside investment. No timeline and no minimum is promised.

5.2 **The calculation.** When the Company distributes, it does so out of Distributable Profit calculated on a consistent basis: revenue, less direct costs, operating expenses, corporation tax, the operating-reserve top-up, and a founder-determined growth-investment allowance. The Founding Member receives **10% of Distributable Profit**. The full method and worked examples are in the *Dividend Calculation Framework*.

5.3 **The reserve protects everyone.** Before any distribution, the Company maintains an operating reserve of at least three months' operating expenses. This reserve is not the Founder's to draw down; it protects the value of every shareholder's interest.

5.4 **Schedule.** Distributions are assessed quarterly, at each quarter close (31 Dec, 31 Mar, 30 Jun, 30 Sep). Where a distribution is made, it is paid within fourteen working days of the relevant reporting date by bank transfer to the Founding Member's nominated account.

5.5 **First scheduled date.** The first assessment and report falls on **15 January 2027** (for the period to 31 December 2026). Thereafter the dates are **15 April, 15 July, 15 October, and 15 January** each year. Whether a payment accompanies any given report depends on whether there is Distributable Profit; in Year 1, while the reserve is being funded, a nil distribution is expected and is normal.

5.6 **No distribution in deficit.** If the Company is operating at a loss in a quarter, no distribution is made.

> **In plain terms.** Four set dates a year you will get a report. On the dates there is profit to share, your 10% is paid within two weeks. The first report is 15 January 2027. Early on it may say "no payment this quarter" — that is the business getting strong, not failing.

## 6. The Founder Circle: reports, communication, and access

6.1 The Founding Member is the first member of the **Founder Circle** — a deliberately small group of Class B holders the Founder may extend over time (see Schedule 1). The designation **Founding Member** is hers alone, and her 10% interest is protected against any later admission by the anti-dilution term in clause 8.

6.2 **Quarterly report.** Four times a year, on the dates in clause 5.5, the Founding Member receives a written founder's report: what the business worked on, what is going well and what is not, the financial summary, the current SSV, and the distribution (if any). It is a genuine account, not investor-relations polish.

6.3 **Annual communication.** Once a year, the fourth-quarter report is expanded into a fuller reflection on the year: what it produced, what was learned, and what the year ahead requires.

6.4 **The Founder Circle portal.** The Founding Member has private, password-protected access to the Founder Circle portal, where her ownership, reports, valuations, distributions, and documents are kept. Access is personal to her and is provided for as long as she holds Shares.

6.5 **Questions answered.** If the Founding Member has a question after any report, she may ask it, and the Founder will answer.

## 7. Quarterly valuation

7.1 The Company is valued each quarter using the **Signal Studio Valuation (SSV)** — a fixed, formula-based measure anchored to trailing revenue, trailing owner earnings, and net cash, defined in the *Valuation Framework*. It deliberately excludes growth projections and counts only performance that has already occurred.

7.2 The SSV is reported to the Founding Member with each quarterly report. The **inaugural valuation** is struck at 31 December 2026 and reported on 15 January 2027.

7.3 The SSV is the reference price for any transaction in the Shares under clauses 11 and 12, unless the Parties jointly commission an independent valuation.

> **In plain terms.** Every quarter you are told what Signal Studio is worth, using a formula based on real money it has actually made — not hopes. That number is also the fair price if shares are ever bought back.

## 8. Anti-dilution — your 10% is protected

8.1 The Founding Member's interest **shall not be diluted below 10%**. If at any time the Company issues, allots, or grants further shares, options, or other equity (whether to employees, investors, or anyone else), the Company shall **at the same time issue to the Founding Member, at nil cost, sufficient additional Class B Shares to restore her holding to 10%** of the enlarged issued share capital.

8.2 This protection is a fundamental term. It cannot be removed or reduced without the Founding Member's prior written consent (see clause 26.2).

8.3 The only event in which the percentage may change is one the Founding Member chooses herself — a sale or buy-back of part of her Shares under clauses 11 or 12.

8.4 Where shares carry a nominal (par) value, any nominal value payable on Class B Shares issued to the Founding Member under this clause is paid by the Company or the Founder, so that the restoration of her 10% is genuinely at no cost to her.

> **In plain terms.** No matter how many new shares the company creates, your slice stays at 10%, and you never pay a cent to keep it there. You can only ever go below 10% if you decide to sell some yourself.

## 9. Vesting

9.1 The Shares are **fully vested on Completion**. There is no vesting schedule, no cliff, and no performance condition.

9.2 The Shares are **not subject to forfeiture**. They cannot be taken back, reduced, or cancelled by the Company or the Founder for any reason, save a transaction the Founding Member herself enters into under this Agreement.

> **In plain terms.** This is a gift, not a salary you have to earn over time. It is fully yours today and stays yours.

## 10. Transfer restrictions

10.1 The Shares are **personal to the Founding Member** and may not be sold, transferred, charged, pledged, or otherwise disposed of, except:

(a) back to the Company or the Founder under clauses 11 or 12;

(b) on death, under clause 16;

(c) to a member of the Founding Member's immediate family, with the Founder's prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld), the transferee taking the Shares subject to this Agreement.

10.2 Any purported transfer outside clause 10.1 is void.

10.3 This restriction protects the Company from its ownership ending up, by accident, with a stranger, a competitor, or a third party in the event of a dispute.

## 11. Right of first refusal

11.1 If the Founding Member wishes to sell any of her Shares to a third party, she must **first offer them to the Company, and then to the Founder**, by written notice stating the number of Shares and the price.

11.2 The reference price is the relevant percentage of the most recent SSV (clause 7), unless a higher bona fide third-party offer exists, in which case that price applies.

11.3 The Company or the Founder has **30 days** to accept. If neither accepts, the Founding Member may sell to the named third party within 90 days at no less than the offered price, the buyer taking the Shares subject to this Agreement.

---

## 12. Your right to sell back to the Company

12.1 The Founding Member may, at any time, offer some or all of her Shares back to the Company by written notice.

12.2 The price is her percentage of the most recent SSV (clause 7), or an independent valuation if jointly commissioned.

12.3 The Company **shall buy the Shares only if it can do so without materially harming its operations** — that is, without drawing the operating reserve below its three-month target and without compromising the Company's ability to meet its obligations. If the Company cannot responsibly fund the purchase at the time, it may defer or pay in instalments by agreement; the Founding Member is under no obligation to sell, and may withdraw the offer.

> **In plain terms.** If you ever want to turn your shares back into cash, you can offer them to the company at the fair quarterly value. The company will buy them only when it can genuinely afford to — the point is never to put the business (and therefore the rest of your shares) at risk to do it.

---

## 13. Drag-along

13.1 If holders of a majority of the Class A Shares approve an Acquisition to a bona fide third party, the Founding Member shall, if required, **sell her Shares on the same terms** (price per share, in proportion to class rights) as part of that transaction.

13.2 The Founding Member shall not be required to give warranties beyond title to her own Shares and her capacity to sell, and her liability shall be limited to the proceeds she actually receives.

> **In plain terms.** If a buyer wants 100% of Signal Studio, your 10% cannot block the deal — but you are dragged *into the payout*, on the same price as everyone else, and you can never be made to lose more than you receive.

---

## 14. Tag-along

14.1 If the Founder proposes to sell a controlling interest in the Company to a third party, the Founding Member has the right (but not the obligation) to **include her Shares in that sale, on the same terms**, pro rata.

14.2 This ensures the Founding Member is never left behind holding a minority interest after control has passed to someone she did not choose.

---

## 15. Exit — sale of the Company

15.1 On an Acquisition, the Founding Member is entitled to receive **ten percent (10%) of the Net Proceeds** attributable to the equity of the Company, in proportion to and consistently with the rights of all shareholders.

15.2 Payment to the Founding Member is made at the same time and in the same form as payment to the Founder, save for amounts properly held in an agreed escrow or earn-out, which are released to her pro rata as and when released generally.

---

## 16. Death, incapacity, and succession

16.1 The Shares form part of the Founding Member's estate. On her death, they pass under her will or, if none, under the rules of intestacy.

16.2 The Founding Member may name a **single beneficiary** to receive the Shares on her death by written notice to the Company. The named beneficiary (or her estate) takes the Shares **subject to this Agreement**.

16.3 On death, the Company and the Founder have the option — but not the obligation — to purchase the Shares from the estate or beneficiary at the most recent SSV, exercisable within **six months** of the grant of probate, subject to the same affordability test as clause 12.3. The estate or beneficiary may accept or decline; if declined, they retain the Shares subject to this Agreement.

16.4 On the Founding Member's permanent incapacity, her rights under this Agreement may be exercised by her duly appointed attorney or committee.

> **In plain terms.** Your shares are yours to leave to whomever you choose. After you, the company *may* offer to buy them at fair value, but your family can keep them if they prefer.

17.1 If the Company undertakes a public offering of its shares, the Founding Member agrees not to sell or otherwise dispose of her Shares for the customary period required by the underwriters (typically up to 180 days following the offering), on the same basis as applies to the Founder and other shareholders.

Because this is a gift from your son, Irish gift-tax thresholds and reliefs may well mean little or nothing is due — but check with an accountant.

## Schedule 4 — Plain-English glossary

- **Class B / Founder Circle Shares** — your shares. They give you money and information, not control.
- **Dividend** — your share of profit, when there is profit to share.
- **Distributable profit** — what is left after the business has paid its costs, its tax, kept its safety reserve, and set aside what it needs to grow.
- **SSV** — the quarterly value of Signal Studio, worked out by a fixed formula from real numbers.
- **Anti-dilution** — the promise that your 10% stays 10%.
- **Drag-along** — if the whole company is sold, you are paid out with everyone else and cannot hold up the deal.
- **Tag-along** — if the founder sells control, you can sell alongside him on the same terms.
- **Right of first refusal** — if you sell, the company and the founder get first chance to buy.
- **Vesting** — none. Your shares are fully yours from day one.
