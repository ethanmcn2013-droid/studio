# Tax & Valuation Memo — Mam's 10% Gift
## Why this gift carries little or no tax, and how to prove it
## v1.0 — 2026-06-01

> **Status: DRAFT analysis for a DIY gift.** Every euro figure below carries a January 2026 knowledge cutoff. **CAT thresholds and rates change with most Budgets — verify the current figures at revenue.ie before relying on any number here.** This is a reasoned framework and an audit trail, not regulated tax advice. Ethan has chosen to proceed without an accountant; this memo exists to make that choice defensible and documented.

---

## 1. The headline

A gift of 10% of Signal Studio **from Ethan to his mother, made at or near incorporation while the company is pre-revenue**, almost certainly triggers **no Capital Acquisitions Tax and requires no CAT return** — because the value being transferred is a small fraction of the relevant threshold. The single most important variable is **timing**: gift it while it is worth ~nominal, not after revenue creates real value.

---

## 2. Capital Acquisitions Tax (CAT) — the recipient's tax

CAT is a **gift/inheritance tax paid by the person receiving** (Mam), charged at **33%** *(verify)* on the value received **above her lifetime tax-free threshold** for the relevant group.

### Which group?

- Disponer (giver) = Ethan. Beneficiary (receiver) = Mam (Ethan's parent).
- A **parent taking a *gift* from a child** falls in **Group B** *(note: a parent taking an *inheritance* from a child on the child's death can be Group A — but this is a lifetime gift, so Group B applies)*.
- **Group B threshold: €40,000** *(Budget 2025 figure, effective Oct 2024 — VERIFY current value)*. This is a **lifetime cumulative** figure across all Group B gifts/inheritances Mam has ever received from Group B disponers, not per-gift.

### The valuation that matters

CAT is charged on **market value at the date of the gift**. At incorporation (July 2026), Signal Studio is:
- pre-launch of the Sept 1 full launch, with minimal-to-no booked recurring revenue;
- not yet profitable (Owner Earnings ≤ 0);
- holding little more than work-in-progress IP and a small cash balance;
- and Mam's slice is a **10% minority, non-voting, contractually non-transferable** interest — which attracts a **minority discount** and an **illiquidity/non-voting discount** on top.

Even taking the SSV formula's *illustrative* pre-launch Example A (total €52,200 → 10% = €5,220), that example assumes €22k ARR already booked. **At the actual gift date, before the launch, the honest market value of Mam's 10% is realistically in the low hundreds to low thousands of euro** — and a minority/non-voting discount pushes it lower still.

### Conclusion

€5,220 (high end) — or more realistically a few hundred euro — **against a €40,000 threshold** means:
- **No CAT is payable.**
- **No IT38 return is required** — a CAT return is only triggered once cumulative Group B benefits exceed **80% of the threshold (≈€32,000)**. Mam is nowhere near it *(verify she has no prior Group B gifts/inheritances that aggregate — e.g. from Ethan's father's side as a parent... unlikely but confirm)*.
- The **annual small-gift exemption (€3,000 per disponer per year)** can additionally be applied, further reducing the assessable value, though it is barely needed here.

> **This is the keystone of the DIY case.** The professional was insurance against valuation risk. At this valuation, that risk is near zero. Gift now; do not wait until revenue makes the number real.

## 3. Stamp duty — the transfer tax (Model B only)

If you use **Model B** (Ethan takes all shares, then gift-transfers 100,000 B to Mam — see `CONSTITUTION-AND-SHARE-STRUCTURE.md`), the stock transfer is in principle a stampable instrument:
- Stamp duty on shares is **1% of market value** for a gift *(verify)*.
- There is an exemption for instruments where the value does not exceed **€1,000** *(verify the current threshold and conditions)*.
- 1% of a near-nominal value = single-digit to low-double-digit euro, or exempt entirely.

**Action:** value the transferred shares at the documented nominal/near-nominal figure (§4), and either claim the low-value exemption or self-assess and pay the trivial duty via Revenue's stamp duty system. Keep the calculation on file. *(Model A — Mam as original subscriber — avoids stamp duty entirely, but loses the gift narrative; see the constitution memo.)*

## 4. The valuation file — what to write down and keep

If Revenue ever asks "what was the 10% worth when you gave it?", you want a contemporaneous, reasoned answer already on file. Create a one-page **Gift Valuation Note** dated the gift date, recording:

1. **Date of gift** and that it is pre-launch / pre-revenue / pre-profit.
2. **Asset base**: cash €___, no distributable profit, IP work-in-progress, no third-party valuation event (no funding round, no offer).
3. **Nominal value floor**: 100,000 shares × €0.001 = €100.
4. **Going-concern view**: apply the SSV formula's floor logic — the business is worth at least net cash; pre-profit it is valued on recurring revenue alone with a 30% haircut (α=0.7). At ~€0 booked ARR, the revenue term is ~€0; SSV ≈ net cash.
5. **Discounts**: minority interest (non-controlling), non-voting, contractually non-transferable → a material discount to even a pro-rata share of net cash.
6. **Concluded market value of Mam's 10%: €___** (a figure in the low hundreds to low thousands, well under €40,000).
7. **Conclusion**: no CAT payable; no IT38 required; threshold usage negligible.

Keep this with the signed agreement and the share certificate. *(I can draft this Gift Valuation Note as a fill-in template on request.)*

## 5. The trap to avoid: this only works as a *gift*

Everything above depends on the transfer being a **genuine gift for natural love and affection** between family. The moment shares are given **in exchange for work or services** — as is being contemplated for the motion-graphics freelancer — the analysis changes completely: it becomes **income** in the recipient's hands (income tax + USC + PRSI), not a CAT gift. That is a different document and a different tax regime entirely. **Do not fold the freelancer into the gift framework.** See `MOTION-FREELANCER-EQUITY-MEMO.md`.

## 6. Ongoing tax (already handled in the agreement)

- **Dividends** Mam receives are taxable income to her (income tax/USC/PRSI on distributions); agreement clause 20 already puts this on her and provides an annual summary by 31 Jan. Good as drafted.
- **Corporation tax**: the company pays CT on its profits (12.5% trading rate *(verify)*); the SSV/dividend framework already computes Distributable Profit after CT.
- **On a future sale**: Mam's gain over her base cost is subject to **Capital Gains Tax (33%, verify)**. Her base cost is the gift-date value (§4) — another reason the contemporaneous valuation note matters.
