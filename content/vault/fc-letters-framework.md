# Founder Letter System — Framework
## Phase 1 · Letter Architecture
*Version 1.0 — 2026-05-30*

---

## Purpose

These letters accompany the physical package that arrives on September 1. The letter is the first thing the recipient encounters inside the box — before the certificate, before the handbook.

Its job is simple: tell the person what they are holding, and why they specifically are holding it.

It must not feel like a cover letter. It must not feel like a legal notice. It must not feel like a thank-you card. It should feel like a founder, at the moment his company launches, writing privately to one of the five people who mattered before anything existed.

---

## Format Specification

### Page
- Size: A4 portrait
- Paper: 120gsm Conqueror Laid Oyster (or equivalent premium laid correspondence paper)
- Printing: single-sided, single page
- Color: Ink (#1C1917) text on Ivory (#F9F7F2) — no letterhead design, no graphical elements

### Layout
- Left margin: 52mm
- Right margin: 45mm
- Top margin: 48mm
- Bottom margin: 58mm
- Format: blocked (no paragraph indent; space between paragraphs)

### Typography
| Element      | Face               | Size    | Weight  | Leading |
|--------------|---------------------|---------|---------|---------|
| Date         | DM Sans Regular     | 9pt     | Regular | —       |
| Salutation   | Cormorant Display   | 20pt    | Regular | 26pt    |
| Body         | Lora Regular        | 11.5pt  | Regular | 18pt    |
| Paragraph gap| —                   | —       | —       | 8pt     |
| Close        | Cormorant Display   | 16pt    | Regular | —       |

### Elements
- **Date:** top left, DM Sans, 9pt, tracked +0.06em, formatted as: `September 1`
- **Salutation:** `[Name],` on its own line, Cormorant Display 20pt, followed by one blank line
- **Body:** blocked paragraphs, Lora 11.5pt
- **Close:** `Ethan` alone, Cormorant Display 16pt, preceded by one blank line
- **Signature:** handwritten above the printed close — Ethan signs every letter by hand
- **No footer.** No page number. No reference number. No printed date below signature.

### What does not appear
- Signal Studio wordmark — not on the letter itself. The certificate and box carry the brand. The letter is personal.
- Any graphical element — no rule, no divider, no ornament. Whitespace alone signals quality.
- Bold, italic, underline — never. Emphasis lives in sentence structure.
- Bullet points — never.
- PS — the letter ends. A postscript undermines the intentional close.

## Structural Architecture

Every letter has five movements. They are not sections — they are a single continuous piece of writing that moves through five emotional registers.

**1. The moment** — what today is.
Open with the fact: Signal Studio launched today. This is declarative, not celebratory. No congratulatory register. Just the fact, stated plainly, which is more powerful for it.

**2. The context** — brief, honest account of what has been built and how quietly.
The recipient knew some version of this story. Acknowledge that without over-explaining. This is not a pitch — it is a private account for someone who already has context.

**3. The gift** — what the package contains, stated plainly.
The certificate. The class. The percentage. The rights. Stated directly, without diminishing or over-inflating. The handbook is referenced here — not explained.

**4. The why** — why this specific person.
This is the only section that varies meaningfully between letters. It names the specific thing this person gave — not a generic "you've always believed in me" — but the actual, particular thing. For Mam, this is different from a friend. For each friend, it will differ.

**5. The future** — quiet acknowledgement of what comes next.
Quarterly updates. Founder-led. What this means going forward. No promises, no projections. Just: this is how it will work.

**The close** — not a sentence. Just a name. The weight is in everything before it.

## Voice Notes for This Document Type

The letter is the only artifact in this package where Ethan speaks as himself — not as Signal Studio, not as a founder in the abstract, but as a person writing to a person.

This means:
- First person throughout. "I" not "we." "Signal Studio" not "we."
- No euphemism. "You were present when there was no evidence this would work" — not "your support has meant a great deal."
- No inflation. Do not say the share is more than it is. Do not say the relationship is more than it is.
- No diminishment. Do not say "this is just a small token." It is not small. Do not call it that.
- The personalized section must name something specific. If you cannot say what was specific about this person, the letter has not been written yet.

### On length
Each letter should be readable in under two minutes. That is the right length. Any longer and it starts to feel like a document. Any shorter and it lacks the weight the moment deserves.

## Personalization Notes

**Mam:** The distinction is depth and specificity. She gets the Founding Member designation, which means she already occupies a different position. Her letter should reflect that — slower, more personal, naming the specific way she trusted before there was evidence. One sentence in her letter should not be possible to write to anyone else.

**Friends (4):** Each needs one paragraph that is genuinely personal — naming what they specifically gave or were. Generic warmth is worse than nothing. If the letter could be addressed to any of the four, rewrite that paragraph until it cannot.

**What NOT to personalize:** The opening, the gift description, the close, and the forward-looking section remain consistent across letters. Consistency is intentional — it signals that this is a designed experience, not ad-hoc.
