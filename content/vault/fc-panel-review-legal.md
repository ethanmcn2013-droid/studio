## FINDING 1 — CRITICAL · the Effective Date is internally false (Corporate Counsel + Risk)

`AGREEMENT.md` is effective **1 June 2026** and clause 2.2 states *"On the Effective Date the issued share capital of the Company is 1,000,000 shares."* But the company is not incorporated until **July**. On 1 June there is no company and no shares — the clause asserts something untrue, and a grant "with effect from" a date before the shares exist cannot legally complete. The portal cap table repeats "1 June 2026."

**This is the one genuine correctness bug in the package.** A gift of shares completes only when the shares are issued and the transfer is registered — which can only happen post-incorporation.

**Recommended fix (Ethan's decision on the date):** split the timeline cleanly —
- **Agreement Date** — when it's signed, recording binding intention (could stay symbolic, e.g. 1 June, or be the actual signing date).
- **Completion** — incorporation + issue + registration of the Shares (July), when the grant takes legal effect.
- **Reveal** — Sept 1, when Mam is *told* (the emotional moment; legally already done).

**Tax interaction:** Completion should land at/near incorporation in July, while the company is worth ~nominal — *before* any signed venue pilot or booked revenue (see Finding 4). Do not let Completion drift to Sept 1 if revenue starts landing first.

**Decision needed:** which date is the Agreement Date, and confirm Completion = incorporation in July. I'll redraft clauses 2.1/2.2/29.4 to the two-date structure once chosen.

## FINDING 2 — HIGH · execute as a deed (Risk & Governance)

The grant is *for natural love and affection, no consideration*. A gratuitous promise can be unenforceable for want of consideration until executed, and the ongoing covenants Mam most relies on (anti-dilution, buy-back, drag/tag) are exactly the promises you want binding without fresh consideration.

**Executing the agreement as a deed** ("SIGNED and DELIVERED as a DEED") makes it binding regardless of the no-consideration point. The agreement already carries a witness block, so it is deed-ready.

**Decision needed:** upgrade the execution block to deed form? (Strongly recommended; I can implement on a yes.) The gift also becomes practically unassailable the moment the shares are registered in Mam's name — so do both: execute as a deed *and* register promptly.
