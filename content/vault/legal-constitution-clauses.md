# Signal Studio Limited — Constitution: Share-Rights Clauses
## Paste-ready drafting for the company constitution
## v1.0 — 2026-06-01

> **Status: DRAFT for DIY incorporation.** These are the share-capital and class-rights provisions to include in the company's constitution (the public document filed with the CRO via CORE). They are written to sit inside an otherwise standard CA2014 LTD constitution. **Verify section references against the Companies Act 2014 and the current CORE constitution template before filing** (Jan-2026 cutoff). Keep the constitution minimal — the commercial machinery (anti-dilution, buy-back, drag/tag, SSV) lives in `AGREEMENT.md`, *not* here, because the constitution is public. Not regulated legal advice.

## Drafting note — what goes where

- The **constitution** (public) carries only: the two classes, their nominal value, voting, dividend/return-of-capital ranking, variation-of-rights, and a transfer hook. That's it.
- The **shareholders' agreement / deed** (private) carries everything else.
- An LTD under CA2014 has a **single-document constitution**; it adopts most of the Act's provisions by default and only needs to state where it departs from or supplements them. The clauses below are the supplements.

## Clause A — Share capital and classes

> The issued share capital of the Company is divided into two classes:
> (a) **Class A Ordinary Shares** of €0.001 each; and
> (b) **Class B Founder Circle Shares** of €0.001 each.
>
> The Class A and Class B Shares rank equally (*pari passu*) in all respects **except** for the voting rights set out in Clause B. The number of shares of each class in issue from time to time is recorded in the Company's register of members.

## Clause B — Voting

> (1) Each **Class A Share** carries one vote on a poll at any general meeting of the Company.
> (2) The **Class B Shares carry no right to vote** at general meetings, and confer no right to receive notice of or attend general meetings, **save** that the Class B Shares carry a vote on, and only on:
>   (a) any resolution to vary, abrogate, or affect the rights attaching to the Class B Shares (Clause D); and
>   (b) any matter on which the Companies Act 2014 confers a non-excludable right to vote on the holders of those shares.

## Clause C — Dividends and return of capital

> (1) Subject to the Companies Act 2014, the Class A and Class B Shares **rank equally for dividends**, in proportion to the nominal value paid up on them, as and when any dividend is declared.
> (2) On a return of capital, whether on a winding up or otherwise, the surplus assets of the Company available for distribution are applied **equally between the Class A and Class B Shares**, in proportion to the nominal value paid up on them.

## Clause D — Variation of class rights

> The rights attaching to the **Class B Shares** may not be varied, abrogated, or adversely affected except with the **consent in writing of the holders of all of the Class B Shares**, or by a special resolution passed at a separate general meeting of the holders of the Class B Shares. This protection cannot be removed without that same consent.

## Clause E — Pre-emption on allotment (disapplication)

> The statutory pre-emption rights under section 69 of the Companies Act 2014 are **disapplied**, so that the directors may allot shares of either class without first offering them to existing members. *(This permits the Founder to allot freely and allows the nil-cost anti-dilution top-up to the Class B holder under the shareholders' agreement to operate without procedural deadlock.)*

## Clause F — Transfers

> (1) The directors may, in their absolute discretion and without giving a reason, **decline to register** the transfer of any share that is not made in accordance with any shareholders' agreement then in force between the members.
> (2) The Class B Shares are otherwise transferable only as permitted by, and subject to, any such shareholders' agreement.
