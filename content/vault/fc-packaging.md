## Philosophy

The package arrives on September 1. It comes in the post, like any other letter. What makes it different is what happens when it is opened.

The packaging should work in two registers simultaneously: ordinary enough to arrive in the post without telegraphing its contents, and extraordinary enough that the recipient understands immediately — before they have read a word — that someone spent time thinking about this.

The quality of the materials is the message. Not the branding. Not the design complexity. The weight of the paper. The texture of the cloth. The fact that the certificate arrives flat and unfolded. These things say: this was prepared with care.

**The single test:** Does this feel like something the recipient will keep, or something they will throw away? Everything in the specification is chosen to pass that test.

---

## Unboxing Sequence

When the package arrives and is opened, the recipient experiences five distinct moments:

**1. The outer mailer arrives.** Plain. Rigid. No branding on the exterior except a small return address label on the back. The recipient knows it is from Ethan but does not know what is inside.

**2. The presentation box is inside.** Stone-grey bookbinding cloth exterior. One blind-embossed element — "signal studio ·" — near the centre of the lid. No other printing. The box is beautiful before it is opened.

**3. Ivory tissue is the first thing seen.** Loosely laid. A small ivory seal sticker holds it closed — "signal studio ·" in letterpress, stone on ivory. The recipient lifts the tissue.

**4. The wax-sealed envelope is revealed.** Ivory laid paper. Recipient's full name written in black ink by hand. A wax seal in deep graphite on the flap. This is the letter — the first thing to read.

**5. Below the envelope: the certificate and handbook.** The certificate sits flat in a clear archival sleeve, backed by grey board. The handbook lies below it, face up. Together, they feel complete.

## Presentation Box Specification

**Type:** Full-overlap lid setup box (lid covers full depth of base)

**Interior dimensions:** 222 × 312 × 36mm
- 222mm wide × 312mm tall: accommodates A4 certificate (210 × 297mm) with 6mm clearance each side
- 36mm deep: accommodates tissue + envelope (3mm) + certificate in sleeve (3mm) + board (2mm) + handbook (6mm) + breathing room

**Exterior dimensions (approx):** 228 × 318 × 42mm (3mm walls)

**Base material:** 2mm rigid greyboard
**Covering material:** Bookbinding cloth, warm stone grey — recommended: "Skivertex" or "Iris" from Ecological Fibers, Stone/Pewter shade; or Canapetta bookcloth in light grey from Italian suppliers

**Interior lining:** Ivory paper, same Conqueror Laid family or equivalent — glued inside base and lid interior

**Lid treatment:** One blind emboss, centred on lid exterior — "signal studio ·" in DM Sans Light, approximately 80mm wide. No ink, no foil. Impression depth: 0.3–0.5mm.

**Color:**
- Exterior: Stone grey cloth — approximately RGB 180/175/168 (equivalent to Pantone Cool Gray 6 C)
- Interior lining: Ivory — same as Ivory token #F9F7F2

## The Envelope

**Size:** DL (220 × 110mm) — fits A4 letter folded in thirds
**Stock:** Conqueror Laid Oyster, 120gsm
**Addressing:** Recipient's full name, handwritten by Ethan in black India ink (not ballpoint). First name only is acceptable for close relationships (Mam). Full name for all others.
**Flap:** Gummed or self-seal; close with wax seal applied over the flap after sealing
**Wax seal:** Deep graphite sealing wax, 25mm diameter. Die: either a plain round (no mark — just the wax texture) or a simple "S" (single capital letter). Either is correct. Plain round is more restrained.
**Back:** Small "signal studio ·" return address label, DM Sans 8pt, Graphite on Ivory, bottom-left of envelope back. Optional — the wax seal may be sufficient identification.

## Contents Assembly

**Inside the envelope:** The letter (A4, folded in thirds — fold top third down, then fold bottom third up so the letter's opening line faces outward when the envelope is opened)

**Certificate assembly:**
- Print certificate on Conquerant Laid Natural 300gsm
- Slide into clear archival polyester sleeve (A4, acid-free, available from archival supply shops or online)
- Place sleeve face-up on top of grey board backing (2mm grey board, 215 × 305mm)
- The combination slides smoothly into the box

**Handbook:** No additional packaging. It sits face-up below the certificate assembly.

**Assembly order in box (bottom to top):**
1. Handbook (face up, centred)
2. Certificate in sleeve + grey board backing (face up, centred, on top of handbook)
3. Ivory tissue (two sheets, loosely layered — not tight)
4. Tissue seal sticker applied to tissue fold
5. Wax-sealed envelope placed on top of tissue, name-side up

**When lid is closed:** The envelope name is the first thing visible when the lid is lifted.

## Outer Mailer

**Type:** Rigid board mailer (A4+) — protects against bending in post. The presentation box must arrive flat, undamaged, and with the blind emboss intact.

**Size:** A4 rigid mailer, interior approximately 240 × 330mm — allows the box to slide in with minimal movement

**Exterior:** Plain brown/kraft board — no branding. This is what Royal Mail/An Post handles. Premium doesn't need to announce itself on the outside.

**Label:** 
- To: recipient's name and address — printed or handwritten
- From: a small return label — "Signal Studio, [Address]" — bottom-left reverse only

**Seal:** A strip of reinforced tape across the mailer opening. No wax seal on the outer mailer — that is reserved for the inner envelope.

---

## Wax Seal Guide

**Wax:** Use "premium" or "glue gun" sealing wax — not traditional sealing wax, which is too brittle for posting. Post-safe flexible sealing wax melts with a standard glue gun and survives transit.

**Die:** A 25mm brass seal stamp. Options:
- Plain round (no design — just the wax impression itself)
- "S" initial — one uppercase letter in a classic serif
- A simple circle with "SS" — initials only

**Color:** Deep graphite — closest match: "Slate" or "Charcoal" post-safe wax from Artisaire or equivalent.

**Application:**
1. Heat glue gun for 5 minutes
2. Seal the envelope flap
3. Dispense wax onto the flap join — approximately the size of a 2-euro coin
4. Press the die firmly and hold for 8–10 seconds
5. Lift cleanly. The impression should be crisp.

**Timing:** Apply wax seals on the day of posting, not before — the wax can crack if stored.
