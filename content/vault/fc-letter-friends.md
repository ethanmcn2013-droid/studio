## Notes on these letters

Four letters. Each shares the same spine — the opening, the gift description, the forward section, and the close. The spine does not change.

What changes is one paragraph: the why. That paragraph must name something specific to this person. It cannot be generic. If it could be addressed to any of the four, it is not written yet.

No names are confirmed for the four friends. This document provides:
1. The shared spine (finalized)

## Personalized Paragraph — Framework

The personalized paragraph answers one question: **what did this specific person give that no one else gave?**

This is not "thank you for your support" (too generic). This is not "you always believed in me" (too abstract). This is the specific and particular thing.

Possible true answers:
- They were the first person Ethan told about the idea
- They asked the question that clarified something important
- They were present at a specific moment when things were uncertain
- They did not treat the early version with skepticism when skepticism would have been reasonable
- They gave time, feedback, or something material at a point when it mattered
- They modelled something — a way of working, a way of thinking — that shaped how Signal Studio was built
- They created a condition (space, introduction, encouragement at the right moment) without being asked

The instruction: for each of the four friends, Ethan should write one honest sentence about the specific thing. Then the paragraph is built around that sentence.

## Notes on the placeholder paragraphs

These are written to be structurally sound and emotionally honest as templates — but they are deliberately indirect because specific context is not yet available.

When personalizing:
- Keep to three to five sentences maximum
- Name a moment or a quality, not a generic attribute
- Do not use the word "believe" or "support" — both are too abstract
- Do not say "I am grateful" — gratitude is expressed through the entire letter, not a single declaration
- One sentence in the paragraph should be genuinely specific — if it could describe anyone, rewrite it

## On using a single base template for all four

Some might see four nearly identical letters and feel the package is less personal. The counter-argument, and the right one: the constancy of the format IS the design. A deliberate, constructed experience signals intention. What differs is what matters — the specific paragraph — and that specificity will be felt precisely because the rest holds still.

This is how premium publishing, legal correspondence, and private banking work: the form is consistent; the substance inside the form is personal. That contrast is the point.

## Envelope addressing

Each envelope is addressed by hand. Not printed. Not labeled. Handwritten in black ink on Ivory or Stone laid envelope.

Format:
```
[Name]
[Address line 1]
[Address line 2]
[Town / City]
[County / Postcode]
```

No company name. No return address on the face. Signal Studio wordmark, very small, on the envelope flap (back) only — or a plain wax seal in lieu of printed return.
