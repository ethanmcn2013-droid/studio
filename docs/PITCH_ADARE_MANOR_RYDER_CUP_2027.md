## The proposition, in one breath

The 2027 Ryder Cup at Adare Manor will be coordinated by people who do not have time to learn software — a GM, a billionaire philanthropist's office, civil engineers, transit authorities, tour operators, and a village. Signal Studio gives all of them **one calm, shared timeline of the same truth**, each seeing only the altitude they need, opened from a single scan — no logins, no app store, no vocabulary.

We are not pitching a project-management tool. We are pitching **operational elegance**: the same discipline of restraint that defines Adare Manor itself, applied to the eighteen months of work between now and the final putt.

---

## Why this is the right partner

Adare Manor's standard is that the guest never sees the machinery. That is *exactly* Signal Studio's design principle: **everything important, nothing distracting.** Most software gives a venue more — more dashboards, more notifications, more to manage. We give less, on purpose, so the people running the event can hold the whole picture in their head and trust it.

This is a flagship, not a transaction. A public, beautifully made Ryder Cup timeline is the single most credible proof point Signal Studio could carry into the Irish hospitality and events market — and Adare Manor's name on a quiet line of patronage, not a logo wall.

## Five pillars

### 1 — The tactile bridge: physical-to-digital onboarding
The experience begins before a screen is touched. The board is not handed an iPad; they are presented a **Limerick-linen presentation case** holding a single high-weight letterpress card: the Adare Manor centenary crest in gold, and one debossed, minimal QR code.

One scan opens that person's **own secure timeline** on their own phone — no login, no password, no download. The medium *is* the message: this is what zero-barrier software feels like. The first thirty seconds physically demonstrate the product's entire philosophy.

### 2 — One truth, three altitudes: the Perspective Switcher
A world-class timeline adapts to who is looking. A single source of truth, three lenses:

- **The Sovereign View** (executive office / board): strategic milestones, legacy and funding gates, macro hand-offs. None of the day-to-day noise.
- **The Ground View** (engineering, plant hire, rail): girder hoists, concrete pours, platform dimensions, track handovers, road diversions.
- **The Guest View** (VIP attendees, tour operators): arrival schedules, shuttle windows, cruise-liner tides at Foynes, village-readiness milestones.

The point that lands in the room: *one tool serves a philanthropist, a civil engineer, and a VIP guest without overwhelming any of them.*

### 3 — Detail you feel, not detail you see
Luxury software is felt. Physics-based transitions that settle rather than snap. An ambient theme that warms on a wet Limerick afternoon and turns crisp on a bright morning. Optional, low-frequency haptics that give a completed milestone a sense of weight. None of it decorative — all of it in service of calm.

### 4 — The operational cockpit: ambient live-feeds
The timeline wraps the world around the work, so no one opens five other apps:

- **Foynes tide tracker** — real-time Shannon Estuary heights for cruise-liner coordination.
- **Adare ground conditions** — a clean typographic weather readout for the course.
- **National transit line** — live status of the Limerick–Foynes line and the N21.

Environmental context becomes part of the plan, not a separate tab.

### 5 — The centenary legacy: from tool to museum
JP McManus does not build for a weekend; he builds for Limerick. A single toggle transforms the timeline from an **active construction tool** into an **archival history book**: as milestones complete, their cards become photo and audio archives — the crews, the foreman's voice, the bridge going in. After the final putt in September 2027, the timeline stops being a management surface and becomes a permanent, beautiful record of how Limerick prepared for the world.
