# Signal Studio · Venue Copy Brief

**Audience.** Marketing copywriters working on the wedding vertical — venue outreach emails, the `/venues` and `/weddings` pages, the venue one-page PDF, the proof video VO script, any asset that sits in front of a venue operator or a couple.

**The posture.** The venue *pays Signal Studio*. Say that with a straight back. Patronage, not enterprise SaaS. The venue is backing the planning year for every couple it books. The couple workspace is private by default. The venue gets a calmer year.

**Authority.** Banned phrases are pulled from BRAND.md §3 and the 2026-05-16 reality-alignment decision in `studio/docs/shipped-state.md` lines 82–85. When this doc and either of those disagree, the source wins.

---

## Banned phrases (do not use)

These never appear in venue-facing copy. Each entry: the phrase, why it is banned, and the replacement.

### Phrases that hide who pays

| Banned | Why it is banned | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| free for your couples | The venue is paying for the couples. "Free" describes the couple's side but hides the patronage that funds it. Reads as a giveaway. | "The planning layer your couples use, included in the year you book them." |
| with our compliments | Reframes the venue's patronage as a gift from Signal Studio. It is not. The venue is the buyer. This phrase now refers only to founder-issued family/friends comp accounts. | "Included in the founding venue programme." |
| on us | Implies Signal Studio is footing the bill. Signal Studio is not. The venue is. | "Backed by the venue." |
| the gift | Same problem as "with our compliments" — softens the commercial relationship and miscredits who is paying. | "The planning layer the venue backs." |
| 18 months free | Reads as a discount or promotional period. It is neither. The couple gets eighteen months because the venue backed the planning. | "Eighteen months of planning, backed by the venue." |
| no card | Implies Signal Studio is bearing the cost. The couple does not need a card because the venue already paid. Say so. | "No card needed — the venue has already backed the year." |

### Phrases that turn the venue into a software customer

| Banned | Why it is banned | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| seats | Per-seat licensing is enterprise SaaS vocabulary. The venue is not buying access for staff; it is backing the planning year for every couple. | "Couples." Or restate as the venue backing the year, not buying seats. |
| per-user | Same as seats. The unit of value is the couple's planning year, not a user account. | "Per couple, backed annually by the venue." |
| MSA | Master service agreement is enterprise procurement. The venue programme is a one-page annual prepay. | "Annual prepay." |
| license | The venue is not licensing software. It is backing a planning year. | "Backed year." Or "annual patronage." |
| enterprise | The venue is a venue. The 80% audience never calls itself enterprise. | Cut the word entirely. If a tier label is needed, use "Venue Edition". |

### Discount theatre

| Banned | Why it is banned | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| SAVE | All-caps SAVE is conversion theatre. The venue programme is permanence, not a discount. Founding cohort locks €1,500 for life — that is the offer. | "Founding cohort locks €1,500 for life." Or "permanence, not a discount." |
| discount banner | Same posture. The pricing page does not use them. Venue copy must not reintroduce them. | Cut. Lead with the lock and the patronage. |

### SaaS-fluff banned by BRAND.md §3 (high-frequency offenders in venue copy)

| Banned | Why it is banned | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| seamless | Promises a smoothness that no real planning year delivers. Banned everywhere, especially in venue copy. | "Uninterrupted." Or describe the calmer outcome directly. |
| world-class | Empty modifier. Says nothing about the couple's experience. | Cut. State the outcome the venue is backing. |
| cutting-edge | Tech-marketing posture. The venue is not buying tech; it is buying a calmer planning year. | Cut. Or describe the specific layer. |
| transform | SaaS-fluff. Overclaims and reads as marketing theatre. | "Hold." Or describe what changes for the couple's planning week. |
| revolutionize | Same problem, larger. | Cut. |
| unleash | Banned, including in body copy. | Cut. |
| supercharge | Banned. | Cut. |
| delight | UX-qualifier word. Venue copy describes outcomes, not UX feelings. | Describe the outcome. |
| seamlessly | Same as seamless. | Cut, or replace with "without interruption". |

### PM jargon and tech-team-coded vocabulary

| Banned | Why it is banned | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| stakeholder | PM-jargon. The venue and the couple are not stakeholders. They are the venue and the couple. | "The venue." "The couple." |
| sprint | PM-jargon. Wedding planning is not a sprint. | "The planning year." Or the specific window. |
| epic | PM-jargon. | Cut. Describe the actual scope. |
| burndown | PM-jargon. | Cut. |
| Kanban | PM-jargon. | Cut. |
| MVP | PM-jargon in body copy. | "First version." Or describe what shipped. |
| engineering team | The venue does not have one and does not care. BRAND.md §3 names this as a specific ban. | "The people doing the work." Or cut. |

### AI register

| Banned | Why it is banned | Replacement |
|---|---|---|
| AI-powered | The brand position is that AI is ambient, never marketed. | Describe the outcome plainly. |
| AI | Same. The product does not market AI even where it uses it. | Cut. |
| intelligent | Implies the system makes decisions for the user. Signal Studio's discipline is that the decision stays with the user. | "Plain English." Or describe the action. |
| smart | Same. | Cut. |
| automatically prioritizes | The brand explicitly refuses this. Tasks does not auto-sort because sorting is the thinking. | "You sort what matters. The system holds the rest." |

### Three-adjective trios

Any three adjectives in a row, in any order — `modern, simple, beautiful`; `calm, clear, confident`; `private, premium, personal` — are banned. This is BRAND.md's anti-quotable rule. Venue copy is the highest-risk surface for this drift because the temptation to compress the venue's brand into a triple is strong. Resist it. One adjective, one noun, one period.

## What to use instead — the cadence

The brand's working vocabulary in venue copy:

- *Backed.* The venue backs the planning year.
- *Uninterrupted.* The couple experience is uninterrupted.
- *Private.* The couple workspace is private by default.
- *Held.* The venue holds the brand promise; the suite holds the planning.
- *Calm.* The planning is calmer. The year is calmer.
- *Quiet.* The planning is quiet behind the visible day.
- *One.* One workspace, one code, one annual prepay, one founding cohort.

The two umbrella lines that anchor venue copy:

- **Clarity, not configuration.**
- **Everything important. Nothing distracting.**

The one venue-specific operating line:

- **The venue stands behind every couple's planning.**

## When a phrase is not on this list

If a phrase is not on the banned list but feels off, run it through the BRAND.md §3 banned-words list and the `studio/docs/shipped-state.md` reality-anchor. If the phrase implies Signal Studio is the gift-giver, or that the venue is buying software seats, or that the couple is being given something for free — rewrite it. The discipline is that the venue pays Signal Studio and backs the couple's year. Every line in venue copy should be readable through that lens.
