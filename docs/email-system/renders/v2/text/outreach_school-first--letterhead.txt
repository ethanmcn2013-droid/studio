Teachers plan several classes across one school year, and most planning
tools want a database of pupils before they help with any of it. That
trade never sits right, and in a school it should not have to.

Signal Studio works the other way around. A teacher plans subjects,
classes and the shape of the year in a private workspace. What gets
shared is chosen, one thing at a time. Everything else stays the
teacher’s own.

No pupil database. Signal Studio holds nothing about the children in a
class: no names, no accounts, no grades. It is useful to the person
teaching, not curious about the people taught.
