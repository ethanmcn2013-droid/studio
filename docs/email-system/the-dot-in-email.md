# The dot in email · the Brand Warden's note

Date: 2026-07-16. The email-system extension of the brand canon: where
the indigo dot lives in the inbox, and where it pointedly does not.

The dot is the brand's entire colour budget. On product surfaces it
earns attention by scarcity; in email, where every sender screams, the
scarcity is the argument. So the rule is stricter here than anywhere
else in the suite.

**Where the dot lives.**

- The wordmark's period, in every direction, in every email. Drawn in
  type, never an image, so it survives image blocking, dark stock and
  forced high contrast. In dark mode the dot does not lighten: indigo
  `#4F46E5` reads on both papers.
- Hairline: nowhere else. The wordmark's dot is the only indigo on the
  page. Links are ink, the engraved button is ink on paper, the facts
  are ink. One page, one dot. This is the strongest statement of the
  rule and the reason Hairline can never look like anyone else's
  security email.
- Broadsheet: the dot, plus text links. Indigo marks "you can go here"
  and nothing else. The masthead rules are ink, the button is ink, the
  folio is ink. On the late-edition dark stock, links lighten to
  `#A5B4FC` to keep contrast; the dot holds.
- Letterhead: the dot, plus at most one pill. The pill is the letter's
  single warm action and appears only in guided and utility mail;
  founder letters refuse buttons entirely, so on the most personal
  surface the dot returns to being alone. Everything else on the sheet,
  including every link, is ink.

**Where it does not.**

- Never in rules, panels, backgrounds or borders. A rule in indigo is
  decoration wearing the brand's coat.
- Never in the destructive register. Deletion facts are ink and one
  line of print-red; the dot does not appear near consequence.
- Never in imagery for its own sake. The film posters carry the 2px
  indigo provenance rail and the thin play ring because the film system
  locked them (they are the film's identity, not the email's); the
  product still carries one indigo state dot because the product does.
  The email chrome never repeats them.
- Never doubled. If a template ever needs a second indigo element, the
  correct fix is to remove the first.

The audit across all 24 v2 renders confirms the rule holds: Hairline
carries exactly one indigo glyph per email; Broadsheet carries the dot
and its links; Letterhead carries the dot and at most one pill. That
gradient of warmth, one dot, dot plus links, dot plus one action, is
the same argument the suite makes everywhere: colour is attention, and
attention is spent, not sprayed.
