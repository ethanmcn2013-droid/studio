# Elevation charters · one per direction

Date: 2026-07-16. Phase 2 of the v2 craft pass. Rule zero: elevation
increases the distance between the three directions. Each charter states
the thesis pushed one step further, the five craft moves, the refusals,
the button, the dot, the dark interpretation, and the fallback stack.
Sign-offs and dissents are recorded at the end of each charter.

Shared engineering floor (all three, from the ledger, owned by the
Client Engineer): bulletproof VML/mso buttons wherever a button exists;
`role="presentation"` on every layout table; alt text styled as copy;
the dark canvas actually reaching the wrapper cell (CL-09); hold-mode
danger colour corrected (CL-10); `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`
wherever amounts, codes and dates appear; no-break spaces before units
and inside the sign-in code; plain text rebuilt as a designed artifact
per direction (CL-14). Shared editorial floor (Compositor): dates become
data. Fixtures carry ISO dates; each direction owns one date grammar and
formats it in one place. No template writes a date string again.

## Hairline · the bank letter, set properly

**Thesis, pushed.** v1 Hairline was quiet. v2 Hairline is engraved. The
version a Swiss private bank would send: one rule weight, one ink, one
dot, and luxury spent exclusively on spacing. It does not persuade; it
informs, and its confidence is that it never raises its voice.

**Five craft moves.**

1. **Monochrome plus one dot.** Indigo appears exactly once: the
   wordmark's dot. Every link, every action, every accent is ink. The
   deletion email loses its three competing indigo accents (CL-16) by
   losing indigo altogether.
2. **The engraved action.** No filled button, ever (CL-15). The primary
   action is a 1px ink-ruled button: paper field, ink border, ink text,
   4px radius, full tap height. Cheque-book furniture, not software. VML
   underneath so Outlook draws the same quiet box.
3. **The facts column perfected.** Key-value rows in tabular figures,
   one tracked-caps value (0.08em) for every mono label including the
   footer (CL-03), and the code set with a no-break thin gap (CL-20).
   The date grammar is the ledger's: `16 Jul 2026`, abbreviated,
   unspaced by ceremony, everywhere Hairline names a date outside prose.
4. **Baseline discipline.** Every vertical interval divisible by four,
   sections by eight. Measure capped near 62 characters. The h1 stays
   20px: authority through restraint, not size.
5. **Plain text as a bank statement.** Its twin uses hyphen rules, a
   two-column fact layout with aligned gutters, links on their own
   lines, and the same abbreviated date grammar. It should read as if it
   were typed deliberately on the machine in the back office.

**Refuses (that the others allow):** filled buttons, any indigo beyond
the dot, imagery of any kind, captions, the suite tagline, numbered
registers, salutations.

**The dot.** The wordmark period, nowhere else. Its scarcity is the
brand argument.

**Dark.** Ink on near-black: canvas and surface `#141416`, text
`#ececf0`, the same single hairline at `#33333a`, the engraved button's
border lightening with the text. No filled element exists in either
scheme.

**Fallback stack.** Swiss grotesque, Helvetica first after Geist:
`Geist, "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, -apple-system, Arial, sans-serif`;
mono `"Geist Mono", "SF Mono", SFMono-Regular, Menlo, Consolas,
monospace`; `tabular-nums` on every fact. The thesis survives the
fallback fully; this is the direction least dependent on Geist.

**Founder amendment, 2026-07-16 (post-selection).** Links render indigo,
not ink: the indigo budget becomes the dot plus links. Recorded in the
decision file; the charter's button and rule clauses stand unchanged.

**Sign-offs.** Compositor: signed, this is my charter. Client Engineer:
signed, with the note that a ruled button underperforms a filled one for
click-through; accepted because utility mail is not conversion mail.
Brand Warden: signed with enthusiasm; one dot is the whole argument.
Copy Editor: signed. Taste Arbiter: signed; removed one thing already,
the footer tagline it never had.

## Broadsheet · the front page, fully made up

**Thesis, pushed.** v1 Broadsheet had the rules of a newspaper. v2 has
its discipline: one folio grammar, running heads, numbered stories whose
numbers mean something, and photographs cropped like a picture desk
chose them. It is the direction that treats every send as an edition.

**Five craft moves.**

1. **The folio.** One masthead line, one grammar, everywhere: section
   left of the date, full-month uppercase, `SECURITY · 16 JULY 2026`,
   and for editions `THE DISPATCH · NO 2 · AUGUST 2026`. This is
   Broadsheet's date grammar (CL-01): the full month, capitals, in the
   folio; sentence-case written dates in prose.
2. **Numbered stories.** Editorial mail numbers its register: the
   principal story unnumbered (it is the front page), then `02`, `03`
   as mono story numbers with real information: each number is a story,
   never decoration. Utility mail refuses numbers; it has nothing to
   count. Destinations in editorial copy become links (CL-23).
3. **The picture desk.** Stills recropped at email scale to their
   subject (CL-17, CL-19): tight on the product window, no dead field,
   no embedded wordmark inside the photograph (the page already signs
   itself). One caption grammar: 12px mono, sentence case, 0.02em, with
   a hairline sitting between photograph and caption.
4. **The ink button, bulletproofed.** The filled ink button is already
   the direction's voice; it gains VML, a 0.02em tracked label, and
   remains the only filled element on the page. Indigo stays in links
   and the dot; the masthead stays ink.
5. **Plain text as the wire edition.** Heavy rule `====` under the
   masthead line, `----` between stories, numbered items, links on
   their own lines. It should read like copy filed to the wire desk.

**Refuses (that the others allow):** pills and rounded warmth, indigo
actions, sentence-case eyebrows, imagery without captions, letters'
salutations and closings.

**The dot.** The wordmark period in masthead and footer, and the indigo
of text links. Never a rule, never a background.

**Dark.** The late edition: same grammar on inverted stock. Canvas
`#1a1a1c`, the heavy rule prints `#ececf0`, the hairline `#3a3a3f`, the
ink button flips to paper with ink text, photographs keep a hairline
frame so light stills read as pictures on dark stock (CL-11).

**Fallback stack.** The Compositor argued for a Georgia-adjacent serif
fallback to keep the editorial register. The Brand Warden refused: most
recipients would then receive a different brand, not a different
register. **Arbiter ruling: grotesque, chosen deliberately** ·
`Geist, -apple-system, "Segoe UI", Roboto, "Helvetica Neue", Arial,
sans-serif`, `tabular-nums` in folios, numbers and amounts. Honest note:
the display h1's -0.03em tracking is tuned for Geist; in fallback the
masthead rules, folio and numbering carry the thesis, and they survive
everywhere. Compositor's dissent recorded, not actioned.

**Sign-offs.** Compositor: signed, dissent on the serif fallback noted
above. Client Engineer: signed. Brand Warden: signed. Copy Editor:
signed, and claims the story numbers. Taste Arbiter: signed; removed
the tagline from the masthead footer's left column so the folio pair
carries it alone once per page.

## Letterhead · more of a letter

**Thesis, pushed.** v1 Letterhead was a sheet with warm type. v2 is a
letter: dated like one, signed like one, with its enclosure named like
one. All warmth lives in the salutation, the measure, the signature and
the enclosure; the sheet itself stays undecorated forever.

**Five craft moves.**

1. **The letter dates itself in words.** Letterhead's date grammar
   (CL-01) is the written date, sentence case, top right of the sheet:
   `16 July 2026`. Not mono, not uppercase: it is part of the letter,
   set in the text face at 13px ink-soft. Letterhead nearly abolishes
   its mono register: only the enclosure line keeps it.
2. **The signature is the design element.** Name at 19px 600, role and
   direct address grouped beneath at solid 20px leading, a consistent
   closing line above. The founder's name gets the typographic weight
   v1 gave the h1.
3. **The enclosure.** The poster is treated as an enclosed photograph:
   written caption above (as locked in the film brief), hairline frame,
   and beneath it the letter's one mono line: `ENCL · FILM-VEN · 60
   SECONDS`. Poster art is re-set at display scale so its smallest type
   clears an 11px-at-render floor (CL-18).
4. **One warm action.** The indigo pill survives as the single indigo
   element beside the dot, and only in guided and utility mail; founder
   letters keep refusing buttons. The raw-link fallback and secondary
   actions set in ink underline (CL-16). VML under the pill.
5. **Plain text as a typed letter.** No rule characters at all: spacing
   and indentation only, the salutation and signature intact, the
   enclosure line last. It should read like a letter typed by a person
   who types well.

**Refuses (that the others allow):** masthead rules, uppercase
datelines, mono eyebrows in the body, numbered registers, ink-filled
buttons, footers inside the sheet beyond the reason line.

**The dot.** The wordmark period on the sheet, and one pill when the
mail must carry an action. Nothing else.

**Dark.** The room goes dark; the letter does not. Canvas `#161618`,
sheet holds paper, ink stays ink, the destructive red stays `#b91c1c`
(CL-10 fixed by charter), and only the postal line outside the sheet
lightens. This is the strongest statement of the three darks and the
cheapest: paper is the brand.

**Fallback stack.** Humanist order, the platform faces first after
Geist: `Geist, -apple-system, "Segoe UI", "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica,
Arial, sans-serif` (SF on Apple clients, where most venue and school
correspondents read mail), `tabular-nums` in the fact rows. The letter
register survives fully in fallback; measure and signature do the work.

**Sign-offs.** Compositor: signed; the written date is the move I care
about. Client Engineer: signed, flagging that sheet-on-canvas plus
radius degrades first in old Outlook, accepted and documented. Brand
Warden: signed. Copy Editor: signed, and claims the enclosure line.
Taste Arbiter: signed; removed one thing, the duplicate address (the
sheet's reason line no longer repeats hello@, the postal line owns it).

## The distance test

After elevation the three must be further apart than v1. The charters
guarantee it structurally: Hairline is the only monochrome direction and
the only one with no imagery; Broadsheet is the only one with a folio,
numbered stories and a filled ink action; Letterhead is the only one
with a written date, a salutation, a signature block and an enclosure
line, and the only one whose dark mode holds paper. Where v1 shared a
date grammar slot, v2 has three deliberate grammars. Where v1 shared one
button in three colours, v2 has three constructions: engraved rule,
filled ink, indigo pill.
