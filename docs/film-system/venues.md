## Audience

Irish wedding venues that already care about their wedding experience:
owner-run country houses, estate venues, and hotel wedding operations that
sell on consideration, not volume. The film speaks to the venue as a
business, but the story it tells is the couple's planning experience, because
that experience is the thing the venue is buying on behalf of its couples.
This is not venue-management software and the film never frames it as such.

**Locked**

## Buyer

The venue owner or general manager. One person who can sign alone, pays
€1,500 per venue per year prepaid, and answers to the venue's reputation.
The buyer watches this film after a founder email; the decision the film
supports is "take the meeting", not "buy".

**Locked**

## User

Two users, deliberately separated:

- The venue's wedding or events manager and coordinator receive the benefit
  (fewer repeated questions, fewer confused threads) but administer nothing.
  The venue team runs nothing, configures nothing, supports nothing.
- The couple is the experienced audience: they do the planning inside Signal
  Studio under the venue's quiet sponsorship. The film shows their
  experience, because that is what the venue stands behind.

**Locked**

## Influencer or internal champion

The wedding or events manager. They feel the repeated-question load daily and
can tell the owner "this is the part of our year that hurts". The film's
tension beat is written so this person recognises their own inbox. They are
not the buyer and the film asks nothing of them.

**Locked**

## Job to be done

For the venue: extend the standard of care the venue already delivers on the
day into the months of planning before it, under the venue's name, with zero
operational load. For the couple inside the story: get the planning out of
threads and into one calm place.

**Locked**

## Existing tension

The wedding day is considered down to the minute. The planning year is not.
Couples plan with a shared doc and a thread, confusion becomes repeated
questions, and the confusion reaches the venue's team as admin. The venue's
quality is judged on months it currently has no way to stand behind.

**Locked**

## Main promise

"Make the planning feel as considered as the day." The venue's name stands
quietly over a planning experience of the same standard as the day itself,
and nothing lands on the venue team to run.

**Locked**

## Trust boundary

Two boundaries, both shown as product behaviour, never asserted:

1. **The couple's private work stays private from the venue.** Notes are
   private by design; only creator-approved extracts leave Notes. The film
   shows the couple's note staying put while the approved extract moves.
2. **The venue's presence is quiet.** The venue name appears as a small
   eyebrow in the couple's workspace. No venue logo, no branding takeover.
   The couple's plan stays the point.

**Locked**

## Reasons to believe

1. All four products are shipped; the film shows real shipping UI at
   deterministic human speed (Signal's briefing card as a designed prop, per
   system §12).
2. The shared object visibly travels the whole path: note, task, timeline,
   briefing. Care becomes clarity, on screen, in one motion.
3. The eyebrow shot: the venue's name, small, real, legible.
4. The boundary shot: the private note that does not move.
5. Nothing for the venue team to run is demonstrated by absence: no admin
   surface ever appears, because none exists for the venue.

standard, and it costs us nothing to run". The feeling at the closing card is
quiet recognition, the same register as the product: that is what happens,
and this is the considered version of it.

## Proposed product-screen sequence

1. **Notes** (real UI): a couple's note in a wedding workspace, venue eyebrow
   at top. The note contains the guest-count thinking plus one personal line
   that will visibly stay behind.
2. **Notes → Tasks** (real UI): the creator-approved extract leaves Notes;
   the task "Send final guest count to catering" arrives with owner and
   date. Rail on the active row. The shared span "Final guest count to
   catering" is live text, never rasterised (V05 lesson).
3. **Timeline** (real UI): public view, the wedding plan legible to someone
   who did not build it, the count row present, rail preserved.
4. **Signal** (designed prop, impressionistic): a briefing card with three
   lines in the shipping prose register.
5. **Eyebrow close** (real UI): tight composition on the venue name above
   the workspace. The single most important venue shot.

All scenes: fixed camera, 1920×1080 product-window composition, geometry
recorded in the ledger, still holds of at least 0.4s, continuity per V05.

## Voiceover working draft · 60s primary

"A couple books your venue. The day will be considered, down to the minute.
The months before it usually are not.

Signal Studio gives every couple you sponsor one calm place. It starts as a
private note.

The final guest count becomes a task, with an owner and a date.

The same count sits on a timeline the caterer can open, no account needed.

And on the right mornings, a short briefing: the few things that need
attention.

[silence over the boundary card]

Your name sits at the top, quietly. The couple's plan stays the point. There
is nothing for your team to run."

silences intact. The no-VO variant holds on the cards alone.

## Open decisions

1. **Price in the film.** Recommendation: no price card in any cut, no
   spoken price in the 60s and 30s; the 90s master may speak €1,500 in its
