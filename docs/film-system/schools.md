## Audience

Teachers in post-primary schools who plan several classes across one school
year, and the school leadership that would need to approve anything a school
adopts. The film's story is the teacher's planning year; the institutional
audience watches to confirm one thing, that nothing about pupils enters the
product.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Buyer

Institutionally, the principal or deputy principal: the approver who answers
for data protection and procurement. No motion toward this buyer is
permitted today. The decision's reasoning stands: there is no school buyer
who can sign alone, and procurement runs on institutional timescales. Any
future commercial shape (school licence, department purchase, teacher-paid
personal use approved by the school) is undesigned and must not be implied
by the film.

**Blocked (segment-sequencing-2026-05 forbids school outbound, demos, and pilots; review 2026-08-16)**

## User

The teacher. One adult account, planning classes: schemes of work, term
sequences, trips, mock exams, parent evenings. Pupils are never users,
never named, never present in the product. The teacher's classes are the
unit of planning: "3rd Year Geography", not thirty children.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Influencer or internal champion

The teacher who already runs the year on a personal system: the diary, the
drive folder, the laminated term plan. They would champion a tool that
respects two things at once, their private planning and their school's
data caution. The film is written so this teacher could forward it to a
principal without a single line needing defence.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Job to be done

Run several classes across one school year in one calm place: keep honest
planning notes private, hold the term's commitments with dates, publish to
the class or the parents only what the teacher chooses, and see the few
things that need attention each week without anything watching anyone.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Existing tension

A teacher's year lives in a diary, a shared drive, and the walk between
classrooms. The tools that offer to help are either surveillance-shaped
(dashboards over pupils) or institutional systems built for administration,
not planning. The teacher needs direction across the year; every offered
tool answers with monitoring instead.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Single-minded proposition

**Signal Studio lets a teacher plan several classes across one school year
in one calm place, with zero pupil data.** The product needs no pupil names,
accounts, emails, grades, or attendance, because the teacher plans classes,
not pupils.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Main promise

"Plan the classes, not the pupils." The working primary line. The year gets
direction; nobody gets watched.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Trust boundary

The central behaviour of the entire film, shown three ways:

1. **Private teacher work stays the teacher's.** Honest planning notes
   (including candid ones about how a class is going) live in Notes and
   never leave unless the teacher sends them; only creator-approved
   extracts move.
2. **Selective publication.** What the class or the parents see is chosen,
   item by item. The film shows the choosing moment: the teacher publishes
   the term plan to the class page, and everything else visibly stays
   behind.
3. **Zero pupil PII, by design, not by policy.** The product contains no
   pupil database to protect because it never asks for one. The frame never
   shows a child's name because the product has nowhere to put it.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment; the boundary itself is product truth and does not move)**

## Reasons to believe

1. The shared object visibly travels the teacher's path: a private planning
   note becomes tasks with dates, the year sits on one timeline, and the
   teacher publishes the spring term plan to the class page by choice.
2. Absence as proof: across every frame there is no roster, no gradebook,
   no attendance view, no pupil column. The film's discipline is that the
   viewer can look for them and not find them.
3. The briefing shows explainable attention: "The field trip is three weeks
   out and the bus is not booked." About the work, never about a person.

**Provisional (creative territory held pending the segment-sequencing amendment)**

## Claims that must not be made

- **No pricing claims at all.** No price, no tier, no edition, no "free for
  teachers", nothing commercial in any cut, on the poster, or on any page.
- No claim that pupil data is "protected", "secured", or "compliant": those
  framings imply the data exists. The claim is that the data is never
  collected.
- No claim of school pilots, school customers, or institutional approval.
- No GDPR or legal-compliance marketing language; compliance posture is not
  a marketing asset, and under-18 data language in a film would imply a
  product surface that must not exist.
- No pupil names, faces, or stand-in child imagery of any kind, including
  fictional fixture data. Fixtures name classes and events only.
- No claim about grade outcomes, behaviour, or classroom results.

**Locked**
