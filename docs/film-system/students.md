# FILM-STU · Signal Studio for Students · Without the Group Chat Mess

Status: creative brief written 2026-07-16 · overall film **Provisional**
Film ID: FILM-STU · Scene series: SU##
Ledger: to be created as `FILM-STU review ledger` before the first scene review
System: `docs/film-system/film-system.md` (this brief inherits it in full)

Plain statement of the film's standing, up front: **production should not
start before the weddings wedge proves out.** `segment-sequencing-2026-05`
(Active, review 2026-08-16) binds sequencing to venues first, and its point 4
names students directly: "Teachers & students = audience truth in BRAND.md
§2.1 only. No outbound, no demos, no pilots, no dedicated paid push, no
segment landing page built on population grounds." The student campaign is
queued behind weddings. This brief exists so that when the gate opens, the
film can move to storyboard without reopening strategy. It is decision-ready,
not production-approved.

The academic-module versus society tension, resolved explicitly: this film is
about the **assessed academic group project inside a semester**. Society and
committee use is a real, different story with an unresolved commercial shape,
and it is documented below as a separate future film, Blocked. The two are
not blended, because a film that speaks to both speaks to neither: the
academic story has a grade, a module, and a fixed deadline; the society story
has ownership, handover, and membership questions the product's commercial
terms have not answered.

## Audience

Students carrying an assessed group project: final-year undergraduates and
postgraduates whose grade routes through three or four other people. Per
`segment-sequencing-2026-05`, the postgraduate and professional student is
the stronger fit; the general undergraduate defaults to free tools before
the voice can land. The film speaks to the person the project routes
through: the one who ends up carrying it.

**Provisional (segment activation is gated behind the weddings wedge; the undergrad versus postgrad weighting may shift the campaign framing at activation)**

## Buyer

The student, self-serve. Student Edition is €9.99 a year with a verified
student email, full Pro tier. One person decides alone, for less than the
cost of a takeaway, and invites the group. There is no institutional buyer
in this film and no lecturer motion.

**Provisional (the price is set but the verification method is not ratified; the buying moment cannot be fully scripted until it is)**

## User

The project group: three to five students on one assessed module. One
creates the workspace; the others join and work. Secondary user: the same
student across their own semester, because the timeline that holds the group
project also holds their other deadlines.

## Influencer or internal champion

The organised group member: the one who currently makes the shared doc,
chases the thread, and carries the merge the night before. The film's
opening tension is written in their experience so they recognise themselves
in the first four seconds. They are the workspace creator and the growth
loop's engine.

**Locked**

## Job to be done

Get the assessed group project out of the group chat: decisions kept,
work named and dated, the semester visible, and the few things that need
attention surfaced without anyone having to police the group.

**Locked**

## Existing tension

Group work disappears into chats and late-night catch-up. Decisions sink
under new messages, deadlines surface late, and one person quietly carries
the project because nobody can see who owns what. The evidence line, from
the segment record (`content/hq/segments/students.md`): the core message is
"Group projects without the group chat mess."

**Locked**

## Single-minded proposition

Recommended and adopted here as the principal acquisition proposition:
**Signal Studio takes the assessed group project out of the group chat: one
module clear, the whole semester visible.** Private notes, connected tasks,
fixed dates, and Signal surfacing the few things that need attention.

**Provisional (adopted as the working proposition; confirm against the first real student cohort at campaign activation, after the weddings wedge gate)**

## Main promise

"Group projects without the group chat mess." One module clear, the whole
semester visible, nobody carrying it alone.

**Provisional (the line is the recorded segment core message and the working primary line; final ratification belongs to campaign activation)**

## Trust boundary

Your own notes stay yours. Rough thinking, half-formed drafts, and honest
observations about the group live in Notes and never leave unless you send
them; only creator-approved extracts move. The film shows the messy draft
staying private while the finished piece becomes a shared task. This matters
to students specifically: group tools that expose everything create
performance, not work.

**Locked**

## Reasons to believe

1. The shared object visibly travels: a private note about the survey
   results becomes the task "Write up the results section", with a name and
   a Friday date, then a semester timeline entry, then a briefing line.
2. The timeline is one page the whole group can open; nobody asks "where is
   the plan" because the plan is the artifact.
3. The briefing shows three lines, not thirty: attention without policing.
4. The boundary shot: the rough note that did not move.

**Locked**

## Desired emotional movement

From the low-grade dread of the unread group chat to the relief of a
project that is visible: from "I am carrying this alone and cannot prove it"
to "everyone can see the work, and I did not have to become the group's
manager to get there".

**Locked**

## Messaging hierarchy

1. The group chat is where the project goes to get lost.
2. One calm place: notes that stay yours, tasks with names and dates, one
   semester timeline, a briefing that speaks only when something needs
   attention.
3. Your rough thinking stays private; only what you send moves.
4. One module clear, the whole semester visible.
5. One action: join the waitlist.

**Locked**

## Voiceover working draft · 60s primary

"A group project runs on a group chat, until it doesn't. Decisions sink.
Deadlines surface late. One person ends up carrying it.

Signal Studio gives the group one calm place. Rough thinking starts as a
private note, yours until you send it.

What you send becomes a task, with a name on it and a date.

The whole semester sits on one timeline everyone can open.

[silence over the boundary card]

One module, clear. The whole semester, visible. No one carrying it alone."

- Building for the general undergraduate: `segment-sequencing-2026-05` warns

  everyone in the lecture hall.
- Blending society use into the academic story would dilute both; this brief
  forbids it and the review gate should check for drift.
- Producing before the weddings wedge proves spends finish-quality effort on

**Locked**

## Open decisions

1. **Student verification method.** Not ratified. Recommendation: decide it
   before the landing page ships, because "with a verified student email"
   is only claimable once a mechanism exists. Alternatives: (a) verified
   student email domain check, consequence: cheap, imperfect coverage of
   students without institutional email; (b) a third-party verification
   service, consequence: coverage and cost, plus a new supplier; (c) honour
   system at €9.99, consequence: simplest, but the price then needs no
   "verified" claim at all and the copy changes. Decision named: founder
   ratifies the student verification method.
2. **Society and committee film.** A separate future film or cutdown, not
   this one. Blocked pending founder decisions on workspace ownership and
   handover between committee years, pricing (who pays, the society or the
   member), and member scope. Recommendation: do not open this until the
   academic film has shipped and taught us the register. Consequence of
   blending instead: one film that persuades neither audience.
3. **Campaign activation.** The film's production green light is the
   weddings wedge proving out, per `segment-sequencing-2026-05`, reviewed
   2026-08-16. Recommendation: hold production until then; keep this brief
   current so the lead time from green light to storyboard is zero.
