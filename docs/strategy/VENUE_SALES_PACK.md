## Purpose

This is the copy source for the first venue-facing sales asset. It should become a one-page PDF and a short web/Notion-style leave-behind, but the copy must work even as plain text.

The asset has one job: make a premium venue owner/operator understand why the Venue Edition is worth a conversation.

It does not need to explain every product. It does need to make the buyer feel the planning noise, understand the mechanic, and believe the venue's name belongs on the couple's planning experience.

---

## One-Page PDF Copy

### Header

**Signal Studio Venue Edition**

The planning layer premium venues give every couple after booking.

### Lead

A couple books your venue. Everyone is warm. Then the planning year starts.

Questions already answered in the welcome pack. Supplier details scattered across emails. Spreadsheet versions that stopped being current. It is not the couple's fault. They are managing one of the most complex personal projects most people ever take on, usually with a thread and a shared document.

That confusion reaches your team as admin.

Signal Studio gives each couple one calm place to plan, with your venue's name in a quiet line at the top.

### What The Couple Gets

| Layer | Plain-English role |
| --- | --- |
| Notes | Where ideas, decisions, and meeting notes start. Private until shared. |
| Tasks | Where the actual work lives: venue, suppliers, guest list, final week, decisions, risks. |
| Timeline | The readable plan they can forward to family, suppliers, and planners without asking anyone to log in. |
| Signal | A short morning briefing when something needs attention. Quiet on the days that do not. |

### What The Venue Does

1. Pay once a year.
2. Hand a code to each couple after booking.
3. Nothing else to run.

The couple never sees a price. They get eighteen months of Signal Studio because your venue backed their planning.

### Why It Matters

| Venue pain | Signal outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Repeated questions | One plan answers the things couples keep asking. |
| Scattered supplier details | Notes and tasks keep decisions tied to the work. |
| Planner/couple/venue drift | Timeline gives everyone the same readable picture. |
| Coordinator inbox noise | Signal surfaces attention before it becomes another chase. |
| Brand experience drops after booking | The venue stays quietly present throughout planning. |

### Pricing

Venue Edition is prepaid annually.

| Venue shape | Annual fee |
| --- | --- |
| Every venue | EUR1,500 |

Founding venues: the first fifteen venues lock EUR1,500/year for as long as they stay.

This is not a discount that expires. It is the founding position.

### Close

Fewer confused couples. Fewer repetitive emails. A better planning experience under your venue's name.

**Ask:** a short venue conversation.

## Objection Cards

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Our couples already have planners." | Good planners benefit from one readable source of truth. This does not replace the planner; it gives the planner, couple, suppliers, and venue a calmer shared picture. |
| "We do not want to run software." | You do not. The venue hands out codes. Signal Studio supports the product. The venue is the sponsor, not the admin. |
| "What if couples do not use it?" | Nothing breaks. The couples who do use it get a materially calmer year, and the venue loses nothing on couples who ignore the code. Usage is learned through the founding cohort. |
| "Is this visible to us?" | The venue name is visible to the couple. The couple's private workspace is not visible to the venue unless the couple chooses to share a Timeline or artifact. |
| "Why annual prepay?" | Because the couple should never see a card form or monthly prompt from the venue's gift. One annual payment makes the couple experience seamless. |
| "Why not per couple?" | The venue is not buying seats. It is backing the planning year for every couple it books. Flat annual pricing keeps the mechanic simple. |

## Implementation Explanation

```
Implementation is deliberately small.

1. Signal Studio creates your venue sponsor record.
2. Signal Studio issues a batch of wedding codes.
3. You send each couple one plain-text note with their code.
4. The couple opens signalstudio.ie/redeem/[CODE].
5. They sign in or create an account.
6. Their wedding workspace opens with your venue's name attached.
7. Signal Studio handles product support.

Your team does not manage users, seats, passwords, billing, or dashboards.
```
