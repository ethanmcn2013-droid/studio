# Venue FAQ And Objections

Status: private answer bank - 2026-05-27
Owner: founder
Boundary: internal use only until a real venue conversation proves which objections matter. No public FAQ is required yet.

---

## Answer Standard

| Standard | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Respect the venue team. | Signal is never positioned as a replacement for coordinators, planners, or service. |
| Answer plainly. | No defensive copy. If the answer is not good enough, improve the product or offer. |
| Keep the buyer/user split clear. | Venue pays. Couple uses. Venue has nothing to run. |
| Do not overpromise. | Founding cohort means founding cohort, not hidden scale. |

---

## Objection Bank

| Objection | Answer | Use when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Our couples already have planners. | Good planners benefit from one readable source of truth. Signal does not replace the planner; it gives the planner, couple, suppliers, and venue a calmer shared picture. | The venue hears this as overlap with planning services. |
| We do not want to run software. | You do not run it. The venue pays once, hands out codes, and Signal Studio supports the product. The venue is the sponsor, not the admin. | The buyer worries about operational lift. |
| What if couples do not use it? | Nothing breaks. The couples who use it get a materially calmer year; the venue loses nothing on couples who ignore the code. Usage is learned through the founding cohort. | The buyer wants adoption certainty before proof exists. |
| Can we see the couple workspace? | The couple workspace is private by default. The venue sees what the couple chooses to share: timeline, updates, and the artefacts that belong in the planning relationship. | The buyer asks about privacy and oversight. |
| Why annual instead of per couple? | The venue is not buying seats. It is backing the planning year for every couple it books. Annual prepay keeps the couple experience seamless and keeps pricing away from the couple. | The buyer asks for per-couple pricing. |
| Who else uses it? | This is the founding cohort. The early venues are shaping the motion before there is a case study, which is why the first fifteen lock the founding price. | The buyer asks for social proof before it exists. |
| We are too busy right now. | That is fair. The right next step is not a demo; it is a calmer proof asset and one short conversation when planning bandwidth exists. | The venue has interest but no immediate time. |

## Founder Talk Track

```text
The simple version is that Signal does not ask the venue to become software people.

The venue pays once a year. Each couple gets a code after booking. The couple opens one calm workspace with your venue's name quietly present. Your team does not manage seats, dashboards, passwords, or billing.

The value is the middle stretch: fewer repeated questions, fewer stale documents, and one readable plan the couple can share with the people around the wedding.
```
